By mid-2025, Palestinian health authorities reported over **60,000 people killed** in Gaza, of whom nearly one-third were children[reuters.com](https://www.reuters.com/world/middle-east/how-many-palestinians-has-israels-gaza-offensive-killed-2025-03-24/#:~:text=)[reuters.com](https://www.reuters.com/world/middle-east/how-many-palestinians-has-israels-gaza-offensive-killed-2025-03-24/#:~:text=A%20new%20update%20released%20by,were%20under%2018). (For context, this far exceeds the 8,000 Bosnian Muslim victims of the 1995 Srebrenica massacre, which was deemed genocide by an international tribunal[theguardian.com](https://www.theguardian.com/commentisfree/2025/jul/24/israel-genocide-gaza#:~:text=There%20is%20little%20doubt%20that,tribunal%20found%20to%20constitute%20genocide).) The United Nations and independent researchers believe even this number is an undercount, as thousands of bodies remain unrecovered under rubble[reuters.com](https://www.reuters.com/world/middle-east/how-many-palestinians-has-israels-gaza-offensive-killed-2025-03-24/#:~:text=The%20numbers%20do%20not%20necessarily,thousand%20bodies%20are%20under%20rubble). According to U.N. data, **nearly 70% of those killed have been women and children**[reuters.com](https://www.reuters.com/world/middle-east/how-many-palestinians-has-israels-gaza-offensive-killed-2025-03-24/#:~:text=Purchase%20Licensing%20Rights%20%2C%20opens,new%20tab) -- clearly indicating that the victims are overwhelmingly non-combatant members of the protected group (Palestinian civilians). Such a **mass killing of a specific ethnic/national group**, in and of itself, satisfies one of the genocidal acts listed in Article II(a) of the Genocide Convention[en.wikipedia.org](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Genocide_Convention#:~:text=,the%20group%20to%20another%20group). It is important to note that genocide does *not* require attempting to kill every member of a group; destroying a substantial part of the group *"in whole or in part"* is sufficient under the Convention[en.wikipedia.org](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Genocide_Convention#:~:text=,the%20group%20to%20another%20group). In Gaza, entire family lineages have been wiped out -- a Reuters analysis found over **1,200 families completely obliterated** (no survivors) by the bombing campaign as of early 2024[reuters.com](https://www.reuters.com/world/middle-east/how-many-palestinians-has-israels-gaza-offensive-killed-2025-03-24/#:~:text=A%20Reuters%20examination%20in%20March,one%20family%20of%2014%20people). This level of destruction of family units and community structure is consistent with genocidal targeting.

**Deliberate infliction of life-threatening conditions** is another genocidal act (Article II(c)), and observers note that Israel's actions have created conditions in Gaza that are *"calculated to bring about [the group's] physical destruction"*[en.wikipedia.org](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Genocide_Convention#:~:text=,the%20group%20to%20another%20group). In the very first days of the war, Israel's Defense Minister Yoav Gallant announced a policy of **"complete siege"** on Gaza: *"no electricity, no food, no fuel"* would be allowed in[timesofisrael.com](https://www.timesofisrael.com/liveblog_entry/defense-minister-announces-complete-siege-of-gaza-no-power-food-or-fuel/#:~:text=Defense%20Minister%20Yoav%20Gallant%20says,fights%20the%20Hamas%20terror%20group). Gallant bluntly declared, *"We are fighting human animals and we are acting accordingly."*[timesofisrael.com](https://www.timesofisrael.com/liveblog_entry/defense-minister-announces-complete-siege-of-gaza-no-power-food-or-fuel/#:~:text=Defense%20Minister%20Yoav%20Gallant%20says,fights%20the%20Hamas%20terror%20group) This statement dehumanizing Gazans as "animals" -- and justifying measures to starve and freeze the population -- rang alarm bells globally, as **dehumanization** is a classic precursor to genocide (it removes moral constraints for killing a group)[theguardian.com](https://www.theguardian.com/commentisfree/2025/jul/24/israel-genocide-gaza#:~:text=brutal%20dictatorship%29,and%20women%2C%20children%20and%20infants%E2%80%9D). The siege indeed plunged Gaza into darkness and desperate humanitarian crisis. Over nearly two years of war, **1.9 million people (about 90% of Gaza's population) have been displaced** from their homes, many sheltering in overcrowded camps[lto.de](https://www.lto.de/recht/hintergruende/h/gaza-is-israel-committing-genocide-assessments-international-law-experts#:~:text=2025). By mid-2025, roughly **half a million people faced famine-like conditions**, and one-third of Gazans went for days without food[lto.de](https://www.lto.de/recht/hintergruende/h/gaza-is-israel-committing-genocide-assessments-international-law-experts#:~:text=2025). Children began dying from malnutrition and preventable disease as the health system collapsed. An international monitoring group warned in July 2025 that a *"worst-case scenario of famine"* was unfolding in Gaza[reuters.com](https://www.reuters.com/world/middle-east/how-many-palestinians-has-israels-gaza-offensive-killed-2025-03-24/#:~:text=2005%2C%20according%20to%20data%20from,Israeli%20human%20rights%20organisation%20B%27Tselem). In short, Israeli forces have **"deliberately deprived [Palestinians] of food, water and medicine"** -- which a consortium of genocide scholars (IAGS) identified as a key component of the genocidal policy in Gaza[aljazeera.com](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2025/9/3/why-have-leading-experts-declared-israels-war-on-gaza-a-genocide#:~:text=,generational%20families%2C%20and). This tactic -- starvation of civilians as a method of warfare -- is not only a war crime; it is also evidence of an intent to destroy a population "in part" by making life unsustainable. Kenneth Roth, former director of Human Rights Watch, noted that Israel's **siege and bombardment tactics** (such as leveling neighborhoods with 2,000-pound bombs and attacking targets despite foreseeable massive civilian casualties) show a *"disregard for Palestinian civilian life"* so extreme that it supports an inference of genocidal intent[theguardian.com](https://www.theguardian.com/commentisfree/2025/jul/24/israel-genocide-gaza#:~:text=genocide). He pointed out that *"devastating [Gaza's] neighborhoods,"* *"blocking access to food and necessities,"* and confining survivors in shrinking, primitive encampments are effectively **destroying the conditions of life** for Gazans[theguardian.com](https://www.theguardian.com/commentisfree/2025/jul/24/israel-genocide-gaza#:~:text=Although%20many%20of%20the%20dead,Palestinians%20as%20they%20seek%20food)[theguardian.com](https://www.theguardian.com/commentisfree/2025/jul/24/israel-genocide-gaza#:~:text=Meanwhile%2C%20Israel%20has%20imposed%20a,conditions%20are%20believed%20to%20have) -- precisely the kind of act Article II(c) was meant to capture.

**Systematic destruction of infrastructure and social institutions** further underscores the claim. Gaza's physical and social fabric has been decimated. By mid-2025, Israeli bombardment had **destroyed 70--90% of Gaza's housing infrastructure**, along with hospitals, schools, places of worship, and markets[theguardian.com](https://www.theguardian.com/commentisfree/2025/jul/24/israel-genocide-gaza#:~:text=camps%20that%20it%20regularly%20moves,death%20toll%20in%20indirect%20deaths)[aljazeera.com](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2025/9/3/why-have-leading-experts-declared-israels-war-on-gaza-a-genocide#:~:text=the%20group%20to%20another%20group). The U.N. Office of the High Commissioner for Human Rights reported that entire districts have been turned to rubble, and the remaining population is *"deprived of healthcare, education and other basic rights"* as a result[theguardian.com](https://www.theguardian.com/world/2025/jul/28/israel-committing-genocide-in-gaza-say-israel-based-human-rights-groups#:~:text=The%20reports%20detailed%20crimes%20including,education%20and%20other%20basic%20rights). The International Association of Genocide Scholars resolution specifically noted Israel's **"indiscriminate and deliberate attacks against the civilians and civilian infrastructure (hospitals, homes, commercial buildings, etc.) of Gaza"**, as well as acts like *torture, arbitrary detention, sexual violence*, and *killing of entire multi-generational families*, as evidence that **all the constitutive acts of genocide are present**[aljazeera.com](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2025/9/3/why-have-leading-experts-declared-israels-war-on-gaza-a-genocide#:~:text=Among%20the%20Israeli%20crimes%20the,of%20Gaza%E2%80%9D)[aljazeera.com](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2025/9/3/why-have-leading-experts-declared-israels-war-on-gaza-a-genocide#:~:text=,injuring%20more%20than%2050%2C000%20children). Destruction of the means for the group's survival -- housing, food supply, medical care -- is tantamount to destruction of the group itself over time. The Genocide Convention drafters included these indirect methods (like creating lethal living conditions) exactly because genocide can be incremental and need not resemble the rapid mass slaughter of, say, the Rwandan genocide. In Gaza, the **cumulative toll of months of bombardment and blockade** has created what one Israeli NGO calls *"sociocide"* -- the annihilation of Palestinian society in Gaza, not only its individual members[theguardian.com](https://www.theguardian.com/world/2025/jul/28/israel-committing-genocide-in-gaza-say-israel-based-human-rights-groups#:~:text=In%20reports%20published%20on%20Monday%2C,irreparable%20damage%20to%20Palestinian%20society).

**Hate rhetoric and incitement** from Israeli leaders bolster the case that the violence is not an unfortunate byproduct of war but is *group-directed*. International tribunals have held that **direct and public incitement to commit genocide** is itself a punishable offense (Article III(c) of the Genocide Convention) and such rhetoric is a strong indicator of genocidal mentality. Israeli officials at the highest levels have made statements effectively characterizing *all* Gazans as the enemy. Besides Gallant's "human animals" comment, Israeli President **Isaac Herzog** argued that *"[Gazans are] an entire nation out there that is responsible"* for Hamas -- saying the idea that any Palestinian civilians were "not involved" is *"absolutely not true"*[timesofisrael.com](https://www.timesofisrael.com/a-blood-libel-herzog-says-icj-twisted-my-words-to-support-unfounded-contention/#:~:text=By%20signing%20up%2C%20you%20agree,to%20the%20terms). He implied that Gazan civilians collectively bore guilt for Hamas's crimes, suggesting there were essentially **no innocent civilians** in Gaza. (Herzog later insisted he was taken out of context[timesofisrael.com](https://www.timesofisrael.com/a-blood-libel-herzog-says-icj-twisted-my-words-to-support-unfounded-contention/#:~:text=Herzog%20had%20said%20at%20that,%E2%80%9D), but the **damage of such language was done** -- many interpreted it as rationalizing indiscriminate collective punishment.) Even more disturbingly, Prime Minister **Benjamin Netanyahu** invoked the Biblical story of **Amalek** in reference to Israel's fight, a story in which God commands the annihilation of an entire nation -- *"men and women, children and infants"*[theguardian.com](https://www.theguardian.com/commentisfree/2025/jul/24/israel-genocide-gaza#:~:text=%E2%80%9Chuman%20animals%E2%80%9D%20%E2%80%93%20not%2C%20as,and%20women%2C%20children%20and%20infants%E2%80%9D). This reference was widely understood as signaling a *"war of extermination"* mindset. In another instance, Finance Minister **Bezalel Smotrich** and National Security Minister **Itamar Ben-Gvir** -- known hardliners -- openly spoke of ejecting all Palestinians from Gaza, effectively advocating **mass displacement/ethnic cleansing** (which can accompany genocide)[theguardian.com](https://www.theguardian.com/commentisfree/2025/jul/24/israel-genocide-gaza#:~:text=That%20is%20a%20better%20way,that%20goal%2C%20as%20has%20Netanyahu). Netanyahu's government indeed prepared plans to **forcibly transfer Gaza's population**: evidence emerged (via Axios reporting) that Israeli envoys sought international help to relocate hundreds of thousands of Gazans to third countries[axios.com](https://www.axios.com/2025/07/18/israel-send-palestinians-gaza-indonesia-ethiopia#:~:text=Why%20it%20matters%3A%20The%20Israeli,labeled%20it%20a%20war%20crime)[axios.com](https://www.axios.com/2025/07/18/israel-send-palestinians-gaza-indonesia-ethiopia#:~:text=,any%20Palestinian%20who%20leaves%20to). This indicates an intent to **eliminate Gaza as a Palestinian-inhabited region**, either by expulsion or by making it uninhabitable. Such an intent aligns with a goal of destroying a group "in part" -- namely, removing Palestinians from Gaza *permanently*. Kenneth Roth has argued that killing enough of a group to terrorize the rest into fleeing is *"a means to the end of ethnic cleansing"*, which **still constitutes genocide** under the Convention[theguardian.com](https://www.theguardian.com/commentisfree/2025/jul/24/israel-genocide-gaza#:~:text=But%20what%20does%20it%20mean,the%20end%20of%20ethnic%20cleansing). (He draws a parallel to the Myanmar military's 2017 attacks on the Rohingya: some 10,000 were killed and 730,000 driven out -- the ICJ is treating this as genocide committed *in order to* ethnically cleanse a region[theguardian.com](https://www.theguardian.com/commentisfree/2025/jul/24/israel-genocide-gaza#:~:text=But%20what%20does%20it%20mean,the%20end%20of%20ethnic%20cleansing).)

Crucially, experts note that **genocidal intent can often be inferred from a pattern of conduct and context, even absent a single explicit "exterminate them all" order**. Genocide seldom announces itself with fanfare; it is recognized by "the **consistent, methodical nature** of atrocities and the rhetoric around them"[lto.de](https://www.lto.de/recht/hintergruende/h/gaza-is-israel-committing-genocide-assessments-international-law-experts#:~:text=Image%3A%20Michael%20A%20BeckerReasons%3A%C2%A0,assess%20Israel%E2%80%99s%20policy%20towards%20Gaza)[lto.de](https://www.lto.de/recht/hintergruende/h/gaza-is-israel-committing-genocide-assessments-international-law-experts#:~:text=As%20of%20August%202025%2C%20there,actions%20in%20Gaza%20do%20not). In Gaza, the pattern over nearly two years has been one of relentless, **disproportionate violence aimed at Palestinians as a group**: entire city blocks razed when one militant might be present, aid convoys bombed, people shot while searching for food[theguardian.com](https://www.theguardian.com/commentisfree/2025/jul/24/israel-genocide-gaza#:~:text=Although%20many%20of%20the%20dead,Palestinians%20as%20they%20seek%20food). Michael A. Becker, an international law professor, observed that by late 2025 the **only reasonable inference** from the totality of evidence is *"a state policy directed at the physical destruction of the Palestinian population of Gaza."*[lto.de](https://www.lto.de/recht/hintergruende/h/gaza-is-israel-committing-genocide-assessments-international-law-experts#:~:text=campaign%20ostensibly%20directed%20against%20Hamas,assess%20Israel%E2%80%99s%20policy%20towards%20Gaza)[lto.de](https://www.lto.de/recht/hintergruende/h/gaza-is-israel-committing-genocide-assessments-international-law-experts#:~:text=As%20of%20August%202025%2C%20there,Gaza%20do%20not%20constitute%20genocide) He noted that Israel's months of siege and military tactics -- combined with the failure to adjust these tactics despite the civilian carnage -- **strongly suggest genocidal intent**, even if earlier in the conflict one might have given benefit of the doubt[lto.de](https://www.lto.de/recht/hintergruende/h/gaza-is-israel-committing-genocide-assessments-international-law-experts#:~:text=campaign%20ostensibly%20directed%20against%20Hamas,assess%20Israel%E2%80%99s%20policy%20towards%20Gaza)[lto.de](https://www.lto.de/recht/hintergruende/h/gaza-is-israel-committing-genocide-assessments-international-law-experts#:~:text=Israel%20bears%20the%20burden%20to,Gaza%20do%20not%20constitute%20genocide). Likewise, Amnesty International's comprehensive investigation (which included hundreds of interviews, satellite imagery analysis, and government statements) concluded that *"for months, Israel has persisted in committing genocidal acts, fully aware of the irreparable harm...it was inflicting on Palestinians in Gaza"*, and that **Israel's intent to physically destroy** this group could be discerned from its actions and repeated warnings it ignored[amnesty.org](https://www.amnesty.org/en/latest/news/2024/12/amnesty-international-concludes-israel-is-committing-genocide-against-palestinians-in-gaza/#:~:text=%E2%80%9COur%20research%20reveals%20that%2C%20for,in%20Gaza%2C%E2%80%9D%20said%20Agn%C3%A8s%20Callamard)[amnesty.org](https://www.amnesty.org/en/latest/news/2024/12/amnesty-international-concludes-israel-is-committing-genocide-against-palestinians-in-gaza/#:~:text=the%20first%20place%20by%20high,charge%20of%20the%20war%20efforts). Amnesty's report emphasizes that **genocidal intent need not be the sole or even primary motive** -- it can exist alongside military goals. In their assessment, Israel's goal of annihilating Hamas *"does not excuse"* the evident intent to also destroy a large part of Gaza's civilian population in the process[amnesty.org](https://www.amnesty.org/en/latest/news/2024/12/amnesty-international-concludes-israel-is-committing-genocide-against-palestinians-in-gaza/#:~:text=Gaza%2C%E2%80%9D%20said%20Agn%C3%A8s%20Callamard)[amnesty.org](https://www.amnesty.org/en/latest/news/2024/12/amnesty-international-concludes-israel-is-committing-genocide-against-palestinians-in-gaza/#:~:text=the%20first%20place%20by%20high,charge%20of%20the%20war%20efforts).

Finally, numerous international voices (outside of Palestinian advocates) have started to label the situation as genocidal. In November 2023, a group of U.N. special rapporteurs warned that *"while States debate terminology -- is it or is it not genocide? -- Israel continues its relentless destruction of life in Gaza"*, urging that *"if we do not end this unfolding genocide, it will end the Palestinian people in Gaza."*[pnberg.com](https://www.pnberg.com/genocide-gaza-insights-nick-berg-shadows-of-tehran/#:~:text=In%20The%20Guardian%2C%20scholar%20Kenneth,action%20even%20as%20atrocities%20unfold)[pnberg.com](https://www.pnberg.com/genocide-gaza-insights-nick-berg-shadows-of-tehran/#:~:text=These%20sources%20collectively%20illustrate%20that,responses%2C%20and%20meaningful%20humanitarian%20action) By late 2024 and 2025, **"genocide"** was being used by prominent genocide scholars, editors of major medical journals (e.g. *The Lancet* spoke of *"several times the official death toll"* dying indirectly[theguardian.com](https://www.theguardian.com/commentisfree/2025/jul/24/israel-genocide-gaza#:~:text=camps%20that%20it%20regularly%20moves,death%20toll%20in%20indirect%20deaths)), and even by some Holocaust survivors and Jewish commentators horrified by the events. The sheer **convergence of independent assessments** -- from academic bodies (IAGS) to NGOs (Amnesty, B'Tselem) to U.N. experts -- all pointing to genocide, lends significant weight to the equivalence argument. As Dr. Melanie O'Brien, president of IAGS, said in 2025: *"This is a definitive statement from experts... that what is going on in Gaza is genocide."*[aljazeera.com](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2025/9/3/why-have-leading-experts-declared-israels-war-on-gaza-a-genocide#:~:text=Eighty,of%20the%20Crime%20of%20Genocide).

In summary, the case for genocide-equivalence rests on these key points: **(1)** The *magnitude* of killing and destruction of Palestinians in Gaza, which is not incidental but systematic and surpasses thresholds of past genocidal events[theguardian.com](https://www.theguardian.com/commentisfree/2025/jul/24/israel-genocide-gaza#:~:text=There%20is%20little%20doubt%20that,tribunal%20found%20to%20constitute%20genocide); **(2)** The *methods* employed (starvation siege, bombing of civilians, razing infrastructure) clearly align with acts described in the Genocide Convention[amnesty.org](https://www.amnesty.org/en/latest/news/2024/12/amnesty-international-concludes-israel-is-committing-genocide-against-palestinians-in-gaza/#:~:text=%E2%80%9CAmnesty%20International%E2%80%99s%20report%20demonstrates%20that,Secretary%20General%20of%20Amnesty%20International); **(3)** The *evidence of intent* seen through both patterns of conduct and explicit hateful rhetoric by officials[theguardian.com](https://www.theguardian.com/commentisfree/2025/jul/24/israel-genocide-gaza#:~:text=brutal%20dictatorship%29,and%20women%2C%20children%20and%20infants%E2%80%9D)[timesofisrael.com](https://www.timesofisrael.com/liveblog_entry/defense-minister-announces-complete-siege-of-gaza-no-power-food-or-fuel/#:~:text=Defense%20Minister%20Yoav%20Gallant%20says,fights%20the%20Hamas%20terror%20group), which treat all Gazans as the enemy "to be dealt with accordingly"; and **(4)** The *recognition by competent experts and organizations* that these facts meet the definition of genocide[aljazeera.com](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2025/9/3/why-have-leading-experts-declared-israels-war-on-gaza-a-genocide#:~:text=The%20IAGS%2C%20a%20500,leading%20association%20of%20genocide%20scholars)[amnesty.org](https://www.amnesty.org/en/latest/news/2024/12/amnesty-international-concludes-israel-is-committing-genocide-against-palestinians-in-gaza/#:~:text=%E2%80%9CAmnesty%20International%E2%80%99s%20report%20demonstrates%20that,Secretary%20General%20of%20Amnesty%20International). Taken together, these factors form what might be called a "signature" of genocide. Advocates say that it is statistically implausible that **so many classic indicators of genocidal campaigns** (dehumanization of the victim group, mass civilian slaughter, deliberate creation of deadly living conditions, leaders framing the assault in terms of eliminating an ethnic group) would all be present *unless* this was, in effect, a genocidal campaign. Each additional point of similarity to known genocides further reduces the likelihood that the resemblance is accidental. By late 2025, many conclude that **the pattern is unmistakable**: Israel's war on Gaza *"appears to meet the very high legal standard of genocide"*[lto.de](https://www.lto.de/recht/hintergruende/h/gaza-is-israel-committing-genocide-assessments-international-law-experts#:~:text=Finally%2C%20and%20notwithstanding%20my%20comments,%E2%80%9D).

Arguments That Israel's Actions Are *Not* Equivalent to Genocide
----------------------------------------------------------------

Despite the above, there is a significant body of opinion -- including official statements by Israel and its allies, as well as some legal experts -- that **disputes the characterization of Israel's Gaza campaign as genocide**. They acknowledge the extreme suffering in Gaza but argue that the specific legal and factual criteria for genocide have not been met. Here are the primary arguments for non-equivalence:

-   **Stated Intent is Against Hamas, Not "Palestinians as such":** The Israeli government insists that its objective in Gaza is *not* to destroy the Palestinian people, but to eliminate the **Hamas** organization (which Israel and others designate as a terrorist group) and to secure the release of Israeli hostages. They point out that Hamas's October 7 attack was itself exceptionally brutal, and assert that Israel is using force in self-defense against the perpetrators of that massacre -- not because of the victims' ethnicity. An article by the American Jewish Committee put it succinctly: Israel's *raison d'etre* for the war is *"the elimination of Hamas and hostage rescue, not the destruction of Gazans simply because they are Palestinian."*[thenewsleaders.com](https://thenewsleaders.com/gaza-conflict-is-a-genocide/#:~:text=As%20part%20of%20my%20research%2C,simply%20because%20they%20are%20Palestinian) From this perspective, Israel lacks the *specific intent* "to destroy [the Palestinian group] as such" -- a **critical distinction**, since genocidal intent must target people *because* of their national/ethnic identity. Israeli leaders have repeatedly said *"we do not war against the Palestinian people"*. In international forums, they emphasize that if Hamas ceased hostilities and released captives, the fighting would stop -- indicating the campaign is contingent on Hamas's actions, not an open-ended war to eradicate Palestinians. President **Isaac Herzog**, after his controversial remarks, stressed that *"innocent Palestinians in Gaza are not considered targets in any way whatsoever"* and that Israel *"operates according to international law"*, even expressing sorrow for the tragedy befalling civilians[timesofisrael.com](https://www.timesofisrael.com/a-blood-libel-herzog-says-icj-twisted-my-words-to-support-unfounded-contention/#:~:text=Herzog%20had%20said%20at%20that,%E2%80%9D)[timesofisrael.com](https://www.timesofisrael.com/a-blood-libel-herzog-says-icj-twisted-my-words-to-support-unfounded-contention/#:~:text=Advertisement). Such statements show Israel is at least professing an intent *not* to wipe out the population. In genocide trials, defendants often protest their innocence by claiming military necessity or other intents; here Israel is *prospectively* claiming its intent is counter-terrorism, not genocide. Those skeptical of the genocide label take this as a sign that **there is no explicit genocidal policy** akin to, say, the Nazi "Final Solution" or the Hutu Power's call to exterminate Tutsis.

-   **No Explicit Plan of Annihilation (and Alternative Motives Exist):** Unlike historical genocides, there has been *no explicit, documented plan* uncovered in which Israeli officials plot to exterminate the Palestinian people. Deniers of the genocide charge argue that Israel's leadership has **political and military motivations** that, however ruthless, differ from genocidal intent. For example, some analysts suggest Israel's strategy is aimed at *"permanently breaking Hamas's governance in Gaza and deterring future attacks,"* or even at *forcibly relocating* Gaza's population as part of a territorial goal (often termed "ethnic cleansing" when an ethnic group is removed, but not necessarily killed). Indeed, **forced displacement** has been openly discussed (e.g. moving Gazans to Egypt or elsewhere)[axios.com](https://www.axios.com/2025/07/18/israel-send-palestinians-gaza-indonesia-ethiopia#:~:text=Why%20it%20matters%3A%20The%20Israeli,labeled%20it%20a%20war%20crime)[axios.com](https://www.axios.com/2025/07/18/israel-send-palestinians-gaza-indonesia-ethiopia#:~:text=,any%20Palestinian%20who%20leaves%20to). While that is a grave violation of international law, it is conceptually distinct from *genocide*, which is about physical destruction of the group, not just expulsion. **Stefan Talmon**, an international law professor, argues that the pattern of Israeli actions in Gaza can be reasonably interpreted as aiming for *vengeance or displacement*, rather than the specific intent to physically annihilate the Palestinians[lto.de](https://www.lto.de/recht/hintergruende/h/gaza-is-israel-committing-genocide-assessments-international-law-experts#:~:text=and%20Kai%20Ambos%20wrote%20on,possible%20inferences%20from%20Israel%27s%20actions). He told *Der Spiegel* that the extremely high **"only reasonable inference"** threshold for intent has not been met: other inferences (e.g. that Israel is trying to depopulate Gaza by making life unlivable, to enable territorial or political goals) are also plausible[lto.de](https://www.lto.de/recht/hintergruende/h/gaza-is-israel-committing-genocide-assessments-international-law-experts#:~:text=and%20Kai%20Ambos%20wrote%20on,possible%20inferences%20from%20Israel%27s%20actions). In the *ICJ*'s jurisprudence, a campaign primarily aiming to *drive out* a population (even if using mass violence) has sometimes been viewed as **ethnic cleansing, not genocide**, if the court believes the *ultimate* goal was removal rather than total physical destruction[theguardian.com](https://www.theguardian.com/commentisfree/2025/jul/24/israel-genocide-gaza#:~:text=In%20its%202015%20decision%20in,an%20inference%20of%20genocidal%20intent). (Critics like Roth contest this parsing, but it has been a factor in ICJ cases such as *Croatia v. Serbia*[theguardian.com](https://www.theguardian.com/commentisfree/2025/jul/24/israel-genocide-gaza#:~:text=In%20its%202015%20decision%20in,an%20inference%20of%20genocidal%20intent).) The upshot: **If Israel's intent is interpreted as trying to force Gazans to flee en masse rather than kill them outright**, then legally that might not qualify as genocidal intent. The *New York Times* noted that thus far Israel "has not tried to kill all Palestinians" in Gaza -- rather it *"has killed enough of them, and imposed conditions of starvation and deprivation that are sufficiently severe, to force them to flee, if things go according to plan."*[theguardian.com](https://www.theguardian.com/commentisfree/2025/jul/24/israel-genocide-gaza#:~:text=That%20is%20a%20better%20way,that%20goal%2C%20as%20has%20Netanyahu) This description aligns more with **ethnic cleansing** (a horrific crime, but one not codified as genocide in treaties) than with a direct genocidal campaign to murder every last member of a group.

-   **High Bar of Proof and Caution in Legal Circles:** International courts and many jurists approach genocide determinations very conservatively. They recall missteps such as early overuse of the term "genocide" (for instance, the term was sometimes invoked in conflicts where the intent was disputed, potentially diluting its meaning). Kenneth Roth, in explaining reluctance, noted an *"unduly narrow understanding"* of genocide among both the public and the ICJ -- many assume genocide is only when a group is *fully* exterminated, as in the Holocaust[theguardian.com](https://www.theguardian.com/commentisfree/2025/jul/24/israel-genocide-gaza#:~:text=compelling%2C%20but%20some%20governments%20and,ICJ)[theguardian.com](https://www.theguardian.com/commentisfree/2025/jul/24/israel-genocide-gaza#:~:text=Israel%20also%20benefits%20from%20a,in%20whole%20or%20in%20part%E2%80%9D). This narrow view can make observers slow to apply the label in more complex cases like Gaza. Moreover, the ICJ's requirement (from the Bosnia v. Serbia case) that genocide intent must be essentially the sole reasonable explanation has historically led the ICJ to *not* find genocide except in clear-cut cases (the ICJ famously did **not** hold Serbia itself responsible for genocide in Bosnia beyond failing to prevent Srebrenica[theguardian.com](https://www.theguardian.com/commentisfree/2025/jul/24/israel-genocide-gaza#:~:text=In%20its%202015%20decision%20in,an%20inference%20of%20genocidal%20intent), despite huge atrocities -- because it saw Serbia's aim as territorial control rather than outright destruction of Bosniaks). Applying that precedent to Gaza, some legal experts say the presence of **concurrent aims** (fighting Hamas, seizing security control of Gaza, etc.) means the bar of "only inference is genocidal intent" isn't met[theguardian.com](https://www.theguardian.com/commentisfree/2025/jul/24/israel-genocide-gaza#:~:text=In%20its%202015%20decision%20in,an%20inference%20of%20genocidal%20intent)[lto.de](https://www.lto.de/recht/hintergruende/h/gaza-is-israel-committing-genocide-assessments-international-law-experts#:~:text=and%20Kai%20Ambos%20wrote%20on,possible%20inferences%20from%20Israel%27s%20actions). For example, **Miguel de Lemos**, an international jurist, opined in August 2025 that there was *"not sufficient evidence publicly available to attain [the] conclusion"* of genocide, acknowledging heinous statements by some Israelis but stopping short of declaring proven intent[lto.de](https://www.lto.de/recht/hintergruende/h/gaza-is-israel-committing-genocide-assessments-international-law-experts#:~:text=match%20at%20L490%20Final%20Judgment%3A,available%20to%20attain%20such%20conclusion). Another expert in the European context phrased it as *"reasonable indications"* of possible genocide, but not yet definitive[lto.de](https://www.lto.de/recht/hintergruende/h/gaza-is-israel-committing-genocide-assessments-international-law-experts#:~:text=match%20at%20L531%20Final%20judgment%3A,%E2%80%9D). This caution illustrates that **within the international legal community, opinion is not unanimous** -- a number of respected scholars were initially very cautious about calling it genocide, and while many have shifted towards that view as the war grinds on, a few still counsel that the evidence of intent is not absolutely conclusive. They often suggest focusing on clear war crimes and crimes against humanity, which are already evident, rather than the more difficult charge of genocide.

-   **Israel and Allies Reject the Term:** On the diplomatic front, virtually all Western governments, while criticizing Israel's excessive force to varying degrees, have **avoided using "genocide"** to describe Gaza. The United States in particular has taken a clear stance. In May 2024, President **Joe Biden** stated flatly: *"What's happening in Gaza is **not** genocide. We reject that."*[reuters.com](https://www.reuters.com/world/biden-whats-happening-gaza-is-not-genocide-2024-05-20/#:~:text=WASHINGTON%2C%20May%2020%20%28Reuters%29%20,Palestinian%20protesters) He was speaking at a public event amid growing protester chants of "genocide" and even accusations dubbing him "Genocide Joe" for supporting Israel[reuters.com](https://www.reuters.com/world/biden-whats-happening-gaza-is-not-genocide-2024-05-20/#:~:text=)[reuters.com](https://www.reuters.com/world/biden-whats-happening-gaza-is-not-genocide-2024-05-20/#:~:text=Biden%20has%20faced%20protests%20at,his%20steadfast%20support%20for%20Israel). Biden instead characterized Israel as the victim of Hamas's terrorism and reiterated that the U.S. stands with Israel's campaign to *"take out...the butchers of Hamas."*[reuters.com](https://www.reuters.com/world/biden-whats-happening-gaza-is-not-genocide-2024-05-20/#:~:text=In%20remarks%20at%20the%20White,and%20took%20hundreds%20of%20hostages) Similarly, U.S. National Security Advisor Jake Sullivan told reporters, *"We do not believe what is happening in Gaza is a genocide. We have been firmly on record rejecting that proposition."*[reuters.com](https://www.reuters.com/world/us/white-house-we-do-not-believe-genocide-is-occurring-gaza-2024-05-13/#:~:text=White%20House%20sees%20no%20genocide,Sullivan%20told%20reporters) These statements reflect not only political alignment with Israel but also the official view that Israel's **intent is not to exterminate**. Israeli officials themselves bristle at the genocide allegation -- President Herzog called the genocide accusations at the ICJ a *"blood libel"*, saying the court twisted his words and that Israel abides by laws of war and *"doesn't target civilians"*[timesofisrael.com](https://www.timesofisrael.com/a-blood-libel-herzog-says-icj-twisted-my-words-to-support-unfounded-contention/#:~:text=%E2%80%9CEven%20on%20a%20personal%20level%2C,unfounded%20legal%20contention%2C%E2%80%9D%20he%20said)[timesofisrael.com](https://www.timesofisrael.com/a-blood-libel-herzog-says-icj-twisted-my-words-to-support-unfounded-contention/#:~:text=Herzog%20had%20said%20at%20that,%E2%80%9D). Indeed, Israeli spokespeople often highlight that the IDF (Israeli Defense Forces) has taken measures such as warning Gazans to evacuate certain areas before attacks, setting up "humanitarian zones" (albeit contested in their safety), and periodically pausing operations for humanitarian ceasefires. While critics argue these measures have been grossly insufficient or even cynical, the **existence of any efforts to spare civilians** is used by Israel to argue *against* a genocidal intent. The American Jewish Committee, in its "5 Reasons" brief, claimed *"Israel's actions demonstrate due care for civilian life"* -- pointing to things like evacuation warnings and medical supplies Israel let in -- and argued that this is incompatible with an intent to destroy the group[thenewsleaders.com](https://thenewsleaders.com/gaza-conflict-is-a-genocide/#:~:text=The%20AJC%E2%80%99s%20third%20reason%20is,ratio%20is%20likely%20still%20large). (Admittedly, independent investigations have challenged these claims of "due care," noting numerous instances of indiscriminate fire and even Israeli use of Palestinian civilians as human shields during ground operations[thenewsleaders.com](https://thenewsleaders.com/gaza-conflict-is-a-genocide/#:~:text=The%20AJC%E2%80%99s%20third%20reason%20is,ratio%20is%20likely%20still%20large)[thenewsleaders.com](https://thenewsleaders.com/gaza-conflict-is-a-genocide/#:~:text=Additionally%2C%20numerous%20Israeli%20soldiers%20and,according%20to%20the%20Associated%20Press). But the broader point stands that Israel *asserts* its conduct is restrained by legal principles, which is not something genocidal regimes typically do.)

-   **Genocide vs. Other Atrocity Crimes:** Some commentators caution that labeling the situation as genocide might overshadow other charges that are more straightforward to prove but equally serious. For instance, even if one were to doubt genocidal intent, **nobody disputes** that Israel's actions constitute grave *war crimes* (e.g. targeting densely populated areas with insufficient distinction) and likely *crimes against humanity* (widespread, systematic attacks on a civilian population). The president of IAGS, while affirming the genocide finding, also noted that fixation on the label *"genocide"* can be a distraction -- by the time you have a "serious and credible concern" about genocide, you already have a humanitarian catastrophe with multiple atrocity crimes occurring[lto.de](https://www.lto.de/recht/hintergruende/h/gaza-is-israel-committing-genocide-assessments-international-law-experts#:~:text=Finally%2C%20and%20notwithstanding%20my%20comments,to%20meet%20the%20very%20high)[lto.de](https://www.lto.de/recht/hintergruende/h/gaza-is-israel-committing-genocide-assessments-international-law-experts#:~:text=Finally%2C%20and%20notwithstanding%20my%20comments,%E2%80%9D). In Gaza's case, even voices hesitant to say "genocide" acknowledge that **inexcusable human suffering and unlawful attacks** are taking place. For example, Professor **Tom Dannenbaum** contends that Israel is *"perpetrating war crimes and crimes against humanity of a gravity equivalent to genocide"* -- such as using starvation as a method of warfare and the mass extermination of civilians as "inhumane acts" -- and he says regardless of the label, third-party states have an urgent duty to stop these crimes[lto.de](https://www.lto.de/recht/hintergruende/h/gaza-is-israel-committing-genocide-assessments-international-law-experts#:~:text=Tom%20Dannenbaum%2C%20Associate%20Professor%20of,School%20of%20Law%20%26%20Diplomacy)[lto.de](https://www.lto.de/recht/hintergruende/h/gaza-is-israel-committing-genocide-assessments-international-law-experts#:~:text=Final%20Judgment%3A%C2%A0,South%20Africa%20brought%20against%20Israel). His nuance is that there is at least a *"serious risk"* of genocide, even if intent is not conclusively proven, and in legal terms that risk triggers obligations to prevent it[lto.de](https://www.lto.de/recht/hintergruende/h/gaza-is-israel-committing-genocide-assessments-international-law-experts#:~:text=Final%20Judgment%3A%C2%A0,South%20Africa%20brought%20against%20Israel)[lto.de](https://www.lto.de/recht/hintergruende/h/gaza-is-israel-committing-genocide-assessments-international-law-experts#:~:text=manner%20available,These%20discernible%20facts%20are%20damning). Thus, one argument is: **even if we don't call it genocide today, we must act as if it could become one** (or already is one in effect), focusing on ending the atrocities rather than debating nomenclature. Some fear that politicizing the term "genocide" -- for example, protesters using it as a slogan ("Stop the Gaza Genocide") -- could backfire if the legal consensus doesn't solidify; it might allow opponents to dismiss legitimate criticism of Israel's conduct as hyperbole. The *Guardian* noted that Western officials have been extremely reluctant to use the term with respect to Israel, sometimes due to political biases (Israel's status as a Holocaust refuge makes the charge particularly jarring)[theguardian.com](https://www.theguardian.com/commentisfree/2025/jul/24/israel-genocide-gaza#:~:text=Israel%20benefits%20from%20a%20halo,benefits%20from%20the%20cognitive%20dissonance)[theguardian.com](https://www.theguardian.com/commentisfree/2025/jul/24/israel-genocide-gaza#:~:text=W%20hy%20is%20it%20so,ICJ). **Kenneth Roth** himself explained that Israel benefits from a *"halo effect"* and cognitive dissonance: people find it hard to reconcile that the Jewish state, founded after genocide, could itself commit genocide[theguardian.com](https://www.theguardian.com/commentisfree/2025/jul/24/israel-genocide-gaza#:~:text=Israel%20benefits%20from%20a%20halo,benefits%20from%20the%20cognitive%20dissonance). This doesn't make it impossible, he argues, but it explains why many hesitate to acknowledge it. In line with that, some commentators (like Nick Berg, writing in *Shadows of Tehran*) argue that the rush to label Israel's actions as genocide has been influenced by emotive rhetoric and political agendas, and that **such a grave accusation requires rigorous proof of intent** to avoid undermining the term's meaning[pnberg.com](https://www.pnberg.com/genocide-gaza-insights-nick-berg-shadows-of-tehran/#:~:text=Academic%20critique%20of%20term%20misuse)[pnberg.com](https://www.pnberg.com/genocide-gaza-insights-nick-berg-shadows-of-tehran/#:~:text=These%20sources%20collectively%20illustrate%20that,responses%2C%20and%20meaningful%20humanitarian%20action). They worry about *"turning a complex conflict into a viral slogan"* without due legal process[pnberg.com](https://www.pnberg.com/genocide-gaza-insights-nick-berg-shadows-of-tehran/#:~:text=This%20article%20doesn%E2%80%99t%20argue%20that,evidence%2C%20are%20immune%20to%20nuance)[pnberg.com](https://www.pnberg.com/genocide-gaza-insights-nick-berg-shadows-of-tehran/#:~:text=In%20The%20Guardian%2C%20scholar%20Kenneth,action%20even%20as%20atrocities%20unfold).

-   **Proportionality of Destruction vs. Total Annihilation:** Another argument raised is about the **proportion of the group destroyed**. Legally, genocide does *not* require the majority of a group to be killed -- even the destruction of a "substantial part" can qualify, especially if it's the part in a specific area. However, skeptics note that as horrific as the death toll in Gaza is, it still represents a minority of the Gazan population (around 3% by some mid-2025 estimates) and an even tinier fraction of the worldwide Palestinian population (well under 1%)[jadaliyya.com](https://www.jadaliyya.com/Details/34248#:~:text=match%20at%20L581%20killed%20by,the%20worldwide%20Palestinian%20population%20today)[jadaliyya.com](https://www.jadaliyya.com/Details/34248#:~:text=since%20Israel%20withdrew%20its%20soldiers,ten%20percent%20of%20the%20pre). By contrast, classic genocides like the Holocaust or Rwanda targeted the *bulk* of the group (the Holocaust killed two-thirds of European Jewry, Rwanda saw ~75% of Tutsis in-country killed). These comparisons are morally fraught -- clearly **the death of tens of thousands of Palestinians, including so many children, is an immense tragedy regardless of percentages**. But those reluctant to use "genocide" argue that Israel's campaign, while unconscionably brutal, has not evidenced an intent to *physically eliminate every Gazan* or Palestinians at large. For instance, Israel has not employed weapons of mass destruction (like chemical or nuclear weapons) that it possesses; nor has it overtly massacred Palestinians in areas it directly controls (e.g. the West Bank), which might be expected if it truly sought to eradicate the people wholesale. They point to the fact that Israel has repeatedly asserted that Palestinians from Gaza *"can leave"* if they choose (Netanyahu said Gazans "should be able to leave... It shouldn't be a prison"[axios.com](https://www.axios.com/2025/07/18/israel-send-palestinians-gaza-indonesia-ethiopia#:~:text=was%20asked%20about%20this%20issue,to%20the%20Israeli%20Prime%20Minister)) -- a statement inconsistent with an intent to kill them all (though critics respond that bombing someone's home then telling them they are "free to leave" their homeland is hardly benevolent). In essence, the contention is that **Israel's endgame appears to be depopulating Gaza or crushing Hamas, not outright genocide of Palestinians as an ethnic group**. If Hamas fighters surrender or are killed and if Palestinians were to evacuate Gaza, Israel presumably would claim its aims satisfied without hunting down every last Palestinian. That conditional nature of the violence (focused on Gaza/Hamas) is, in this view, different from the absolutist, open-ended nature of genocidal campaigns.

Summarizing the non-equivalence arguments: **(1)** Israel's professed *intent* is counter-terrorism, not group extermination, and its leaders explicitly deny any aim to destroy the Palestinian people[thenewsleaders.com](https://thenewsleaders.com/gaza-conflict-is-a-genocide/#:~:text=As%20part%20of%20my%20research%2C,simply%20because%20they%20are%20Palestinian)[timesofisrael.com](https://www.timesofisrael.com/a-blood-libel-herzog-says-icj-twisted-my-words-to-support-unfounded-contention/#:~:text=Herzog%20had%20said%20at%20that,%E2%80%9D). **(2)** The evidence can be interpreted to fit other motives (however reprehensible) like retaliation or ethnic cleansing, meaning genocidal intent isn't the *only* inference[lto.de](https://www.lto.de/recht/hintergruende/h/gaza-is-israel-committing-genocide-assessments-international-law-experts#:~:text=and%20Kai%20Ambos%20wrote%20on,possible%20inferences%20from%20Israel%27s%20actions). **(3)** Legally, genocide is hard to prove and some experts say that high bar hasn't been conclusively cleared yet, urging caution and further evidence-gathering[lto.de](https://www.lto.de/recht/hintergruende/h/gaza-is-israel-committing-genocide-assessments-international-law-experts#:~:text=match%20at%20L490%20Final%20Judgment%3A,available%20to%20attain%20such%20conclusion)[lto.de](https://www.lto.de/recht/hintergruende/h/gaza-is-israel-committing-genocide-assessments-international-law-experts#:~:text=and%20Kai%20Ambos%20wrote%20on,possible%20inferences%20from%20Israel%27s%20actions). **(4)** Major international actors, including Israel's allies, reject the term and maintain that calling it "genocide" without definitive proof of intent risks politicizing the issue[reuters.com](https://www.reuters.com/world/biden-whats-happening-gaza-is-not-genocide-2024-05-20/#:~:text=WASHINGTON%2C%20May%2020%20%28Reuters%29%20,Palestinian%20protesters)[pnberg.com](https://www.pnberg.com/genocide-gaza-insights-nick-berg-shadows-of-tehran/#:~:text=In%20The%20Guardian%2C%20scholar%20Kenneth,action%20even%20as%20atrocities%20unfold). And **(5)** Even absent the genocide label, the focus should remain on stopping the *documented* atrocities (war crimes and crimes against humanity), as these are indisputably occurring and create a humanitarian imperative regardless of nomenclature[lto.de](https://www.lto.de/recht/hintergruende/h/gaza-is-israel-committing-genocide-assessments-international-law-experts#:~:text=Finally%2C%20and%20notwithstanding%20my%20comments,the%20case%2C%20it%20is%20a)[lto.de](https://www.lto.de/recht/hintergruende/h/gaza-is-israel-committing-genocide-assessments-international-law-experts#:~:text=manner%20available,These%20discernible%20facts%20are%20damning).

Conclusion
----------

The question of whether Israel's actions in Gaza amount to **genocide** is both extremely grave and hotly contested. On one hand, the **sheer scale and nature of the violence against Palestinians in Gaza since October 2023 have led many experts and organizations to conclude that the legal definition of genocide has been met**. The systematic killing of tens of thousands of civilians (including a hugely disproportionate number of children and other vulnerable people), the deliberate creation of life-threatening conditions through siege and devastation, the rhetoric framing all Gazans as the enemy, and documented plans to remove Palestinians from their land all mirror the patterns of past genocides[theguardian.com](https://www.theguardian.com/commentisfree/2025/jul/24/israel-genocide-gaza#:~:text=There%20is%20little%20doubt%20that,tribunal%20found%20to%20constitute%20genocide)[aljazeera.com](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2025/9/3/why-have-leading-experts-declared-israels-war-on-gaza-a-genocide#:~:text=,generational%20families%2C%20and). High-profile bodies -- from **Genocide Scholars** to **Amnesty International** to Israel's own leading human rights groups -- have raised the alarm that *"this is genocide"*, demanding it be stopped[aljazeera.com](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2025/9/3/why-have-leading-experts-declared-israels-war-on-gaza-a-genocide#:~:text=The%20IAGS%2C%20a%20500,leading%20association%20of%20genocide%20scholars)[amnesty.org](https://www.amnesty.org/en/latest/news/2024/12/amnesty-international-concludes-israel-is-committing-genocide-against-palestinians-in-gaza/#:~:text=%E2%80%9CAmnesty%20International%E2%80%99s%20report%20demonstrates%20that,Secretary%20General%20of%20Amnesty%20International). In their view, the **intent to destroy, at least in part, the Palestinian population of Gaza is evidenced by the totality of actions and words**, even if Israeli officials couch their goals in terms of fighting Hamas.

On the other hand, **skeptics of the genocide equivalence urge a careful, technical assessment of intent and warn against jumping to the most incendiary label**. They acknowledge that Israel is committing egregious injustices in Gaza, but hold that these may represent ruthless warfare and persecution **short of the special crime of crimes that is genocide**. They point out the lack of an explicit extermination order, the presence of other strategic motivations, and the fact that key international judicial processes have not (yet) ruled it genocide -- emphasizing that genocide has a very high threshold of proof for intent[theguardian.com](https://www.theguardian.com/commentisfree/2025/jul/24/israel-genocide-gaza#:~:text=In%20its%202015%20decision%20in,an%20inference%20of%20genocidal%20intent)[lto.de](https://www.lto.de/recht/hintergruende/h/gaza-is-israel-committing-genocide-assessments-international-law-experts#:~:text=and%20Kai%20Ambos%20wrote%20on,possible%20inferences%20from%20Israel%27s%20actions). Additionally, political realities mean that the genocide label, if misapplied, could undermine credibility and hinder dialogue. Even some who hesitate to use the word "genocide" concur that **from the perspective of the victims, the situation is almost indistinguishable from genocide** -- in terms of suffering -- but they focus on rallying action through clear-cut legal findings of war crimes, etc., without entering a potentially protracted debate over intent.

Ultimately, whether the world calls it *"genocide"* or not, what is happening in Gaza represents an **unprecedented humanitarian and human rights catastrophe**. As one scholar put it, *"even if [Gaza] were not [genocide], it is a situation of inexcusable human suffering that already demands an urgent and uncompromising response, genocide or not."*[lto.de](https://www.lto.de/recht/hintergruende/h/gaza-is-israel-committing-genocide-assessments-international-law-experts#:~:text=Finally%2C%20and%20notwithstanding%20my%20comments,%E2%80%9D) Under the Genocide Convention, states also have a duty to **prevent genocide** once they suspect it might be occurring -- a threshold that arguably has been crossed given the serious risk indicators[lto.de](https://www.lto.de/recht/hintergruende/h/gaza-is-israel-committing-genocide-assessments-international-law-experts#:~:text=manner%20available,These%20discernible%20facts%20are%20damning). In practical terms, the international community should act decisively to halt the atrocities and protect Gaza's civilian population **regardless** of the ongoing legal debate. If and when courts do weigh all the evidence, they will deliver a verdict on the genocide question. Until then, the **evidence gathered so far** -- as outlined above -- will continue to be critical in pressing for accountability. Should it be conclusively determined that Israel's actions were done with genocidal intent, it would mark the first time in history that a nation widely regarded as a democratic state committed genocide -- a sobering reminder that no society is inherently immune from gravely persecuting another. If, alternatively, the intent remains unproven, Israel's actions will still likely be recorded as among the worst atrocities of our era, constituting widespread war crimes and crimes against humanity. In either case, the priority is to end the bloodshed and ensure justice for the victims. The label of *genocide* is more than just a word -- but the world must not let arguments over that label delay the interventions needed to save lives.

Many of these indicators do not themselves constitute genocide legally, but their presence can signal a genocidal trajectory. Since October 2023, multiple human rights groups and experts have been applying such frameworks to Gaza, noting with alarm the prevalence of classic warning signs -- from openly dehumanizing rhetoric to systematic atrocities -- that resemble those of past genocides theguardian.com aljazeera.com . Differing thresholds by organizations: Various institutions have weighed in on whether Gaza meets the genocide criteria, sometimes using slightly different yardsticks: United Nations: U.N. officials use the Genocide Convention's legal definition. In a January 2024 order, the International Court of Justice (ICJ) (seized of a case brought by South Africa) found "plausible" evidence that Palestinians in Gaza face a risk of genocide, citing incendiary statements by Israeli leaders as potential indications of genocidal intent timesofisrael.com . This was a preliminary finding (for provisional measures), not a final judgment, but it underscored the seriousness of the allegations. International Criminal Court (ICC): The ICC's ongoing investigation (opened in 2021) originally focused on alleged war crimes in the Palestinian territories, but by late 2024 the ICC Prosecutor reportedly sought arrest warrants for Israeli officials for war crimes reuters.com . Genocide charges were not initially at the forefront, reflecting the cautious approach of requiring clear evidence of intent. (Notably, U.S. President Joe Biden criticized the ICC Prosecutor's moves and maintained that Israel's actions, while causing enormous civilian harm, do not constitute genocide reuters.com reuters.com .) Genocide Scholars: The International Association of Genocide Scholars (IAGS), a global scholarly body, voted in September 2025 by an overwhelming 86% majority to declare that Israel's policies and actions in Gaza "fulfil the definition of genocide" under the 1948 Convention aljazeera.com . This resolution by 500 experts -- many of whom have studied historical genocides -- is a strong academic signal that, in their view, the criteria for genocide have been met in Gaza. Human Rights Organizations: Groups like Amnesty International and Human Rights Watch initially documented potential war crimes and crimes against humanity in Gaza, but by late 2024 Amnesty International publicly concluded that "Israel has committed and is continuing to commit genocide against Palestinians in the occupied Gaza Strip." In a December 2024 report, Amnesty found evidence of prohibited acts (mass killings, widespread infliction of conditions calculated to destroy the population) carried out with the specific intent to destroy Palestinians in Gaza amnesty.org . Amnesty's Secretary General stated: "Israel has treated Palestinians in Gaza as a subhuman group unworthy of human rights and dignity, demonstrating its intent to physically destroy them... This is genocide. It must stop now." amnesty.org amnesty.org . Leading Israeli human rights groups B'Tselem and Physicians for Human Rights--Israel likewise issued reports in July 2025 accusing Israel of "intentional targeting of Palestinians as a group" and the "systematic destruction of Palestinian society" in Gaza -- explicitly using the word genocide and urging Israel's allies to act to stop it theguardian.com theguardian.com . In sum, any determination of genocide must meet a very high evidentiary threshold. We will now turn to the evidence on both sides of this question: first, the case that Israel's actions are equivalent to genocide, and second, the case that they fall short of genocide (however egregious they may be). Evidence That Israel's Actions Are Equivalent to Genocide A Palestinian mother in Gaza clutches the body of her 4-year-old child, who died of malnutrition and lack of medicine due to the blockade. Since October 2023, Gaza's civilians have suffered mass casualties, starvation, and displacement on a scale that some experts say meets the definition of genocide aljazeera.com lto.de . Since the war began, the toll on the Palestinian population in Gaza has been staggering. By mid-2025, Palestinian health authorities reported over 60,000 people killed in Gaza, of whom nearly one-third were children reuters.com reuters.com . (For context, this far exceeds the 8,000 Bosnian Muslim victims of the 1995 Srebrenica massacre, which was deemed genocide by an international tribunal theguardian.com .) The United Nations and independent researchers believe even this number is an undercount, as thousands of bodies remain unrecovered under rubble reuters.com . According to U.N. data, nearly 70% of those killed have been women and children reuters.com -- clearly indicating that the victims are overwhelmingly non-combatant members of the protected group (Palestinian civilians). Such a mass killing of a specific ethnic/national group, in and of itself, satisfies one of the genocidal acts listed in Article II(a) of the Genocide Convention en.wikipedia.org . It is important to note that genocide does not require attempting to kill every member of a group; destroying a substantial part of the group "in whole or in part" is sufficient under the Convention en.wikipedia.org . In Gaza, entire family lineages have been wiped out -- a Reuters analysis found over 1,200 families completely obliterated (no survivors) by the bombing campaign as of early 2024 reuters.com . This level of destruction of family units and community structure is consistent with genocidal targeting. Deliberate infliction of life-threatening conditions is another genocidal act (Article II(c)), and observers note that Israel's actions have created conditions in Gaza that are "calculated to bring about [the group's] physical destruction" en.wikipedia.org . In the very first days of the war, Israel's Defense Minister Yoav Gallant announced a policy of "complete siege" on Gaza: "no electricity, no food, no fuel" would be allowed in timesofisrael.com . Gallant bluntly declared, "We are fighting human animals and we are acting accordingly." timesofisrael.com This statement dehumanizing Gazans as "animals" -- and justifying measures to starve and freeze the population -- rang alarm bells globally, as dehumanization is a classic precursor to genocide (it removes moral constraints for killing a group) theguardian.com . The siege indeed plunged Gaza into darkness and desperate humanitarian crisis. Over nearly two years of war, 1.9 million people (about 90% of Gaza's population) have been displaced from their homes, many sheltering in overcrowded camps lto.de . By mid-2025, roughly half a million people faced famine-like conditions, and one-third of Gazans went for days without food lto.de . Children began dying from malnutrition and preventable disease as the health system collapsed. An international monitoring group warned in July 2025 that a "worst-case scenario of famine" was unfolding in Gaza reuters.com . In short, Israeli forces have "deliberately deprived [Palestinians] of food, water and medicine" -- which a consortium of genocide scholars (IAGS) identified as a key component of the genocidal policy in Gaza aljazeera.com . This tactic -- starvation of civilians as a method of warfare -- is not only a war crime; it is also evidence of an intent to destroy a population "in part" by making life unsustainable. Kenneth Roth, former director of Human Rights Watch, noted that Israel's siege and bombardment tactics (such as leveling neighborhoods with 2,000-pound bombs and attacking targets despite foreseeable massive civilian casualties) show a "disregard for Palestinian civilian life" so extreme that it supports an inference of genocidal intent theguardian.com . He pointed out that "devastating [Gaza's] neighborhoods," "blocking access to food and necessities," and confining survivors in shrinking, primitive encampments are effectively destroying the conditions of life for Gazans theguardian.com theguardian.com -- precisely the kind of act Article II(c) was meant to capture. Systematic destruction of infrastructure and social institutions further underscores the claim. Gaza's physical and social fabric has been decimated. By mid-2025, Israeli bombardment had destroyed 70--90% of Gaza's housing infrastructure, along with hospitals, schools, places of worship, and markets theguardian.com aljazeera.com . The U.N. Office of the High Commissioner for Human Rights reported that entire districts have been turned to rubble, and the remaining population is "deprived of healthcare, education and other basic rights" as a result theguardian.com . The International Association of Genocide Scholars resolution specifically noted Israel's "indiscriminate and deliberate attacks against the civilians and civilian infrastructure (hospitals, homes, commercial buildings, etc.) of Gaza", as well as acts like torture, arbitrary detention, sexual violence, and killing of entire multi-generational families, as evidence that all the constitutive acts of genocide are present aljazeera.com aljazeera.com . Destruction of the means for the group's survival -- housing, food supply, medical care -- is tantamount to destruction of the group itself over time. The Genocide Convention drafters included these indirect methods (like creating lethal living conditions) exactly because genocide can be incremental and need not resemble the rapid mass slaughter of, say, the Rwandan genocide. In Gaza, the cumulative toll of months of bombardment and blockade has created what one Israeli NGO calls "sociocide" -- the annihilation of Palestinian society in Gaza, not only its individual members theguardian.com . Hate rhetoric and incitement from Israeli leaders bolster the case that the violence is not an unfortunate byproduct of war but is group-directed. International tribunals have held that direct and public incitement to commit genocide is itself a punishable offense (Article III(c) of the Genocide Convention) and such rhetoric is a strong indicator of genocidal mentality. Israeli officials at the highest levels have made statements effectively characterizing all Gazans as the enemy. Besides Gallant's "human animals" comment, Israeli President Isaac Herzog argued that "[Gazans are] an entire nation out there that is responsible" for Hamas -- saying the idea that any Palestinian civilians were "not involved" is "absolutely not true" timesofisrael.com . He implied that Gazan civilians collectively bore guilt for Hamas's crimes, suggesting there were essentially no innocent civilians in Gaza. (Herzog later insisted he was taken out of context timesofisrael.com , but the damage of such language was done -- many interpreted it as rationalizing indiscriminate collective punishment.) Even more disturbingly, Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu invoked the Biblical story of Amalek in reference to Israel's fight, a story in which God commands the annihilation of an entire nation -- "men and women, children and infants" theguardian.com . This reference was widely understood as signaling a "war of extermination" mindset. In another instance, Finance Minister Bezalel Smotrich and National Security Minister Itamar Ben-Gvir -- known hardliners -- openly spoke of ejecting all Palestinians from Gaza, effectively advocating mass displacement/ethnic cleansing (which can accompany genocide) theguardian.com . Netanyahu's government indeed prepared plans to forcibly transfer Gaza's population: evidence emerged (via Axios reporting) that Israeli envoys sought international help to relocate hundreds of thousands of Gazans to third countries axios.com axios.com . This indicates an intent to eliminate Gaza as a Palestinian-inhabited region, either by expulsion or by making it uninhabitable. Such an intent aligns with a goal of destroying a group "in part" -- namely, removing Palestinians from Gaza permanently. Kenneth Roth has argued that killing enough of a group to terrorize the rest into fleeing is "a means to the end of ethnic cleansing", which still constitutes genocide under the Convention theguardian.com . (He draws a parallel to the Myanmar military's 2017 attacks on the Rohingya: some 10,000 were killed and 730,000 driven out -- the ICJ is treating this as genocide committed in order to ethnically cleanse a region theguardian.com .) Crucially, experts note that genocidal intent can often be inferred from a pattern of conduct and context, even absent a single explicit "exterminate them all" order. Genocide seldom announces itself with fanfare; it is recognized by "the consistent, methodical nature of atrocities and the rhetoric around them" lto.de lto.de . In Gaza, the pattern over nearly two years has been one of relentless, disproportionate violence aimed at Palestinians as a group: entire city blocks razed when one militant might be present, aid convoys bombed, people shot while searching for food theguardian.com . Michael A. Becker, an international law professor, observed that by late 2025 the only reasonable inference from the totality of evidence is "a state policy directed at the physical destruction of the Palestinian population of Gaza." lto.de lto.de He noted that Israel's months of siege and military tactics -- combined with the failure to adjust these tactics despite the civilian carnage -- strongly suggest genocidal intent, even if earlier in the conflict one might have given benefit of the doubt lto.de lto.de . Likewise, Amnesty International's comprehensive investigation (which included hundreds of interviews, satellite imagery analysis, and government statements) concluded that "for months, Israel has persisted in committing genocidal acts, fully aware of the irreparable harm...it was inflicting on Palestinians in Gaza", and that Israel's intent to physically destroy this group could be discerned from its actions and repeated warnings it ignored amnesty.org amnesty.org . Amnesty's report emphasizes that genocidal intent need not be the sole or even primary motive -- it can exist alongside military goals. In their assessment, Israel's goal of annihilating Hamas "does not excuse" the evident intent to also destroy a large part of Gaza's civilian population in the process amnesty.org amnesty.org . Finally, numerous international voices (outside of Palestinian advocates) have started to label the situation as genocidal. In November 2023, a group of U.N. special rapporteurs warned that "while States debate terminology -- is it or is it not genocide? -- Israel continues its relentless destruction of life in Gaza", urging that "if we do not end this unfolding genocide, it will end the Palestinian people in Gaza." pnberg.com pnberg.com By late 2024 and 2025, "genocide" was being used by prominent genocide scholars, editors of major medical journals (e.g. The Lancet spoke of "several times the official death toll" dying indirectly theguardian.com ), and even by some Holocaust survivors and Jewish commentators horrified by the events. The sheer convergence of independent assessments -- from academic bodies (IAGS) to NGOs (Amnesty, B'Tselem) to U.N. experts -- all pointing to genocide, lends significant weight to the equivalence argument. As Dr. Melanie O'Brien, president of IAGS, said in 2025: "This is a definitive statement from experts... that what is going on in Gaza is genocide." aljazeera.com . In summary, the case for genocide-equivalence rests on these key points: (1) The magnitude of killing and destruction of Palestinians in Gaza, which is not incidental but systematic and surpasses thresholds of past genocidal events theguardian.com ; (2) The methods employed (starvation siege, bombing of civilians, razing infrastructure) clearly align with acts described in the Genocide Convention amnesty.org ; (3) The evidence of intent seen through both patterns of conduct and explicit hateful rhetoric by officials theguardian.com timesofisrael.com , which treat all Gazans as the enemy "to be dealt with accordingly"; and (4) The recognition by competent experts and organizations that these facts meet the definition of genocide aljazeera.com amnesty.org . Taken together, these factors form what might be called a "signature" of genocide. Advocates say that it is statistically implausible that so many classic indicators of genocidal campaigns (dehumanization of the victim group, mass civilian slaughter, deliberate creation of deadly living conditions, leaders framing the assault in terms of eliminating an ethnic group) would all be present unless this was, in effect, a genocidal campaign. Each additional point of similarity to known genocides further reduces the likelihood that the resemblance is accidental. By late 2025, many conclude that the pattern is unmistakable: Israel's war on Gaza "appears to meet the very high legal standard of genocide" lto.de . Arguments That Israel's Actions Are Not Equivalent to Genocide Despite the above, there is a significant body of opinion -- including official statements by Israel and its allies, as well as some legal experts -- that disputes the characterization of Israel's Gaza campaign as genocide. They acknowledge the extreme suffering in Gaza but argue that the specific legal and factual criteria for genocide have not been met. Here are the primary arguments for non-equivalence: Stated Intent is Against Hamas, Not "Palestinians as such": The Israeli government insists that its objective in Gaza is not to destroy the Palestinian people, but to eliminate the Hamas organization (which Israel and others designate as a terrorist group) and to secure the release of Israeli hostages. They point out that Hamas's October 7 attack was itself exceptionally brutal, and assert that Israel is using force in self-defense against the perpetrators of that massacre -- not because of the victims' ethnicity. An article by the American Jewish Committee put it succinctly: Israel's raison d'etre for the war is "the elimination of Hamas and hostage rescue, not the destruction of Gazans simply because they are Palestinian." thenewsleaders.com From this perspective, Israel lacks the specific intent "to destroy [the Palestinian group] as such" -- a critical distinction, since genocidal intent must target people because of their national/ethnic identity. Israeli leaders have repeatedly said "we do not war against the Palestinian people". In international forums, they emphasize that if Hamas ceased hostilities and released captives, the fighting would stop -- indicating the campaign is contingent on Hamas's actions, not an open-ended war to eradicate Palestinians. President Isaac Herzog, after his controversial remarks, stressed that "innocent Palestinians in Gaza are not considered targets in any way whatsoever" and that Israel "operates according to international law", even expressing sorrow for the tragedy befalling civilians timesofisrael.com timesofisrael.com . Such statements show Israel is at least professing an intent not to wipe out the population. In genocide trials, defendants often protest their innocence by claiming military necessity or other intents; here Israel is prospectively claiming its intent is counter-terrorism, not genocide. Those skeptical of the genocide label take this as a sign that there is no explicit genocidal policy akin to, say, the Nazi "Final Solution" or the Hutu Power's call to exterminate Tutsis. No Explicit Plan of Annihilation (and Alternative Motives Exist): Unlike historical genocides, there has been no explicit, documented plan uncovered in which Israeli officials plot to exterminate the Palestinian people. Deniers of the genocide charge argue that Israel's leadership has political and military motivations that, however ruthless, differ from genocidal intent. For example, some analysts suggest Israel's strategy is aimed at "permanently breaking Hamas's governance in Gaza and deterring future attacks," or even at forcibly relocating Gaza's population as part of a territorial goal (often termed "ethnic cleansing" when an ethnic group is removed, but not necessarily killed). Indeed, forced displacement has been openly discussed (e.g. moving Gazans to Egypt or elsewhere) axios.com axios.com . While that is a grave violation of international law, it is conceptually distinct from genocide, which is about physical destruction of the group, not just expulsion. Stefan Talmon, an international law professor, argues that the pattern of Israeli actions in Gaza can be reasonably interpreted as aiming for vengeance or displacement, rather than the specific intent to physically annihilate the Palestinians lto.de . He told Der Spiegel that the extremely high "only reasonable inference" threshold for intent has not been met: other inferences (e.g. that Israel is trying to depopulate Gaza by making life unlivable, to enable territorial or political goals) are also plausible lto.de . In the ICJ's jurisprudence, a campaign primarily aiming to drive out a population (even if using mass violence) has sometimes been viewed as ethnic cleansing, not genocide, if the court believes the ultimate goal was removal rather than total physical destruction theguardian.com . (Critics like Roth contest this parsing, but it has been a factor in ICJ cases such as Croatia v. Serbia theguardian.com .) The upshot: If Israel's intent is interpreted as trying to force Gazans to flee en masse rather than kill them outright, then legally that might not qualify as genocidal intent. The New York Times noted that thus far Israel "has not tried to kill all Palestinians" in Gaza -- rather it "has killed enough of them, and imposed conditions of starvation and deprivation that are sufficiently severe, to force them to flee, if things go according to plan." theguardian.com This description aligns more with ethnic cleansing (a horrific crime, but one not codified as genocide in treaties) than with a direct genocidal campaign to murder every last member of a group. High Bar of Proof and Caution in Legal Circles: International courts and many jurists approach genocide determinations very conservatively. They recall missteps such as early overuse of the term "genocide" (for instance, the term was sometimes invoked in conflicts where the intent was disputed, potentially diluting its meaning). Kenneth Roth, in explaining reluctance, noted an "unduly narrow understanding" of genocide among both the public and the ICJ -- many assume genocide is only when a group is fully exterminated, as in the Holocaust theguardian.com theguardian.com . This narrow view can make observers slow to apply the label in more complex cases like Gaza. Moreover, the ICJ's requirement (from the Bosnia v. Serbia case) that genocide intent must be essentially the sole reasonable explanation has historically led the ICJ to not find genocide except in clear-cut cases (the ICJ famously did not hold Serbia itself responsible for genocide in Bosnia beyond failing to prevent Srebrenica theguardian.com , despite huge atrocities -- because it saw Serbia's aim as territorial control rather than outright destruction of Bosniaks). Applying that precedent to Gaza, some legal experts say the presence of concurrent aims (fighting Hamas, seizing security control of Gaza, etc.) means the bar of "only inference is genocidal intent" isn't met theguardian.com lto.de . For example, Miguel de Lemos, an international jurist, opined in August 2025 that there was "not sufficient evidence publicly available to attain [the] conclusion" of genocide, acknowledging heinous statements by some Israelis but stopping short of declaring proven intent lto.de . Another expert in the European context phrased it as "reasonable indications" of possible genocide, but not yet definitive lto.de . This caution illustrates that within the international legal community, opinion is not unanimous -- a number of respected scholars were initially very cautious about calling it genocide, and while many have shifted towards that view as the war grinds on, a few still counsel that the evidence of intent is not absolutely conclusive. They often suggest focusing on clear war crimes and crimes against humanity, which are already evident, rather than the more difficult charge of genocide. Israel and Allies Reject the Term: On the diplomatic front, virtually all Western governments, while criticizing Israel's excessive force to varying degrees, have avoided using "genocide" to describe Gaza. The United States in particular has taken a clear stance. In May 2024, President Joe Biden stated flatly: "What's happening in Gaza is not genocide. We reject that." reuters.com He was speaking at a public event amid growing protester chants of "genocide" and even accusations dubbing him "Genocide Joe" for supporting Israel reuters.com reuters.com . Biden instead characterized Israel as the victim of Hamas's terrorism and reiterated that the U.S. stands with Israel's campaign to "take out...the butchers of Hamas." reuters.com Similarly, U.S. National Security Advisor Jake Sullivan told reporters, "We do not believe what is happening in Gaza is a genocide. We have been firmly on record rejecting that proposition." reuters.com These statements reflect not only political alignment with Israel but also the official view that Israel's intent is not to exterminate. Israeli officials themselves bristle at the genocide allegation -- President Herzog called the genocide accusations at the ICJ a "blood libel", saying the court twisted his words and that Israel abides by laws of war and "doesn't target civilians" timesofisrael.com timesofisrael.com . Indeed, Israeli spokespeople often highlight that the IDF (Israeli Defense Forces) has taken measures such as warning Gazans to evacuate certain areas before attacks, setting up "humanitarian zones" (albeit contested in their safety), and periodically pausing operations for humanitarian ceasefires. While critics argue these measures have been grossly insufficient or even cynical, the existence of any efforts to spare civilians is used by Israel to argue against a genocidal intent. The American Jewish Committee, in its "5 Reasons" brief, claimed "Israel's actions demonstrate due care for civilian life" -- pointing to things like evacuation warnings and medical supplies Israel let in -- and argued that this is incompatible with an intent to destroy the group thenewsleaders.com . (Admittedly, independent investigations have challenged these claims of "due care," noting numerous instances of indiscriminate fire and even Israeli use of Palestinian civilians as human shields during ground operations thenewsleaders.com thenewsleaders.com . But the broader point stands that Israel asserts its conduct is restrained by legal principles, which is not something genocidal regimes typically do.) Genocide vs. Other Atrocity Crimes: Some commentators caution that labeling the situation as genocide might overshadow other charges that are more straightforward to prove but equally serious. For instance, even if one were to doubt genocidal intent, nobody disputes that Israel's actions constitute grave war crimes (e.g. targeting densely populated areas with insufficient distinction) and likely crimes against humanity (widespread, systematic attacks on a civilian population). The president of IAGS, while affirming the genocide finding, also noted that fixation on the label "genocide" can be a distraction -- by the time you have a "serious and credible concern" about genocide, you already have a humanitarian catastrophe with multiple atrocity crimes occurring lto.de lto.de . In Gaza's case, even voices hesitant to say "genocide" acknowledge that inexcusable human suffering and unlawful attacks are taking place. For example, Professor Tom Dannenbaum contends that Israel is "perpetrating war crimes and crimes against humanity of a gravity equivalent to genocide" -- such as using starvation as a method of warfare and the mass extermination of civilians as "inhumane acts" -- and he says regardless of the label, third-party states have an urgent duty to stop these crimes lto.de lto.de . His nuance is that there is at least a "serious risk" of genocide, even if intent is not conclusively proven, and in legal terms that risk triggers obligations to prevent it lto.de lto.de . Thus, one argument is: even if we don't call it genocide today, we must act as if it could become one (or already is one in effect), focusing on ending the atrocities rather than debating nomenclature. Some fear that politicizing the term "genocide" -- for example, protesters using it as a slogan ("Stop the Gaza Genocide") -- could backfire if the legal consensus doesn't solidify; it might allow opponents to dismiss legitimate criticism of Israel's conduct as hyperbole. The Guardian noted that Western officials have been extremely reluctant to use the term with respect to Israel, sometimes due to political biases (Israel's status as a Holocaust refuge makes the charge particularly jarring) theguardian.com theguardian.com . Kenneth Roth himself explained that Israel benefits from a "halo effect" and cognitive dissonance: people find it hard to reconcile that the Jewish state, founded after genocide, could itself commit genocide theguardian.com . This doesn't make it impossible, he argues, but it explains why many hesitate to acknowledge it. In line with that, some commentators (like Nick Berg, writing in Shadows of Tehran) argue that the rush to label Israel's actions as genocide has been influenced by emotive rhetoric and political agendas, and that such a grave accusation requires rigorous proof of intent to avoid undermining the term's meaning pnberg.com pnberg.com . They worry about "turning a complex conflict into a viral slogan" without due legal process pnberg.com pnberg.com . Proportionality of Destruction vs. Total Annihilation: Another argument raised is about the proportion of the group destroyed. Legally, genocide does not require the majority of a group to be killed -- even the destruction of a "substantial part" can qualify, especially if it's the part in a specific area. However, skeptics note that as horrific as the death toll in Gaza is, it still represents a minority of the Gazan population (around 3% by some mid-2025 estimates) and an even tinier fraction of the worldwide Palestinian population (well under 1%) jadaliyya.com jadaliyya.com . By contrast, classic genocides like the Holocaust or Rwanda targeted the bulk of the group (the Holocaust killed two-thirds of European Jewry, Rwanda saw ~75% of Tutsis in-country killed). These comparisons are morally fraught -- clearly the death of tens of thousands of Palestinians, including so many children, is an immense tragedy regardless of percentages. But those reluctant to use "genocide" argue that Israel's campaign, while unconscionably brutal, has not evidenced an intent to physically eliminate every Gazan or Palestinians at large. For instance, Israel has not employed weapons of mass destruction (like chemical or nuclear weapons) that it possesses; nor has it overtly massacred Palestinians in areas it directly controls (e.g. the West Bank), which might be expected if it truly sought to eradicate the people wholesale. They point to the fact that Israel has repeatedly asserted that Palestinians from Gaza "can leave" if they choose (Netanyahu said Gazans "should be able to leave... It shouldn't be a prison" axios.com ) -- a statement inconsistent with an intent to kill them all (though critics respond that bombing someone's home then telling them they are "free to leave" their homeland is hardly benevolent). In essence, the contention is that Israel's endgame appears to be depopulating Gaza or crushing Hamas, not outright genocide of Palestinians as an ethnic group. If Hamas fighters surrender or are killed and if Palestinians were to evacuate Gaza, Israel presumably would claim its aims satisfied without hunting down every last Palestinian. That conditional nature of the violence (focused on Gaza/Hamas) is, in this view, different from the absolutist, open-ended nature of genocidal campaigns. Summarizing the non-equivalence arguments: (1) Israel's professed intent is counter-terrorism, not group extermination, and its leaders explicitly deny any aim to destroy the Palestinian people thenewsleaders.com timesofisrael.com . (2) The evidence can be interpreted to fit other motives (however reprehensible) like retaliation or ethnic cleansing, meaning genocidal intent isn't the only inference lto.de . (3) Legally, genocide is hard to prove and some experts say that high bar hasn't been conclusively cleared yet, urging caution and further evidence-gathering lto.de lto.de . (4) Major international actors, including Israel's allies, reject the term and maintain that calling it "genocide" without definitive proof of intent risks politicizing the issue reuters.com pnberg.com . And (5) Even absent the genocide label, the focus should remain on stopping the documented atrocities (war crimes and crimes against humanity), as these are indisputably occurring and create a humanitarian imperative regardless of nomenclature lto.de lto.de . Conclusion The question of whether Israel's actions in Gaza amount to genocide is both extremely grave and hotly contested. On one hand, the sheer scale and nature of the violence against Palestinians in Gaza since October 2023 have led many experts and organizations to conclude that the legal definition of genocide has been met. The systematic killing of tens of thousands of civilians (including a hugely disproportionate number of children and other vulnerable people), the deliberate creation of life-threatening conditions through siege and devastation, the rhetoric framing all Gazans as the enemy, and documented plans to remove Palestinians from their land all mirror the patterns of past genocides theguardian.com aljazeera.com . High-profile bodies -- from Genocide Scholars to Amnesty International to Israel's own leading human rights groups -- have raised the alarm that "this is genocide", demanding it be stopped aljazeera.com amnesty.org . In their view, the intent to destroy, at least in part, the Palestinian population of Gaza is evidenced by the totality of actions and words, even if Israeli officials couch their goals in terms of fighting Hamas. On the other hand, skeptics of the genocide equivalence urge a careful, technical assessment of intent and warn against jumping to the most incendiary label. They acknowledge that Israel is committing egregious injustices in Gaza, but hold that these may represent ruthless warfare and persecution short of the special crime of crimes that is genocide. They point out the lack of an explicit extermination order, the presence of other strategic motivations, and the fact that key international judicial processes have not (yet) ruled it genocide -- emphasizing that genocide has a very high threshold of proof for intent theguardian.com lto.de . Additionally, political realities mean that the genocide label, if misapplied, could undermine credibility and hinder dialogue. Even some who hesitate to use the word "genocide" concur that from the perspective of the victims, the situation is almost indistinguishable from genocide -- in terms of suffering -- but they focus on rallying action through clear-cut legal findings of war crimes, etc., without entering a potentially protracted debate over intent. Ultimately, whether the world calls it "genocide" or not, what is happening in Gaza represents an unprecedented humanitarian and human rights catastrophe. As one scholar put it, "even if [Gaza] were not [genocide], it is a situation of inexcusable human suffering that already demands an urgent and uncompromising response, genocide or not." lto.de Under the Genocide Convention, states also have a duty to prevent genocide once they suspect it might be occurring -- a threshold that arguably has been crossed given the serious risk indicators lto.de . In practical terms, the international community should act decisively to halt the atrocities and protect Gaza's civilian population regardless of the ongoing legal debate. If and when courts do weigh all the evidence, they will deliver a verdict on the genocide question. Until then, the evidence gathered so far -- as outlined above -- will continue to be critical in pressing for accountability. Should it be conclusively determined that Israel's actions were done with genocidal intent, it would mark the first time in history that a nation widely regarded as a democratic state committed genocide -- a sobering reminder that no society is inherently immune from gravely persecuting another. If, alternatively, the intent remains unproven, Israel's actions will still likely be recorded as among the worst atrocities of our era, constituting widespread war crimes and crimes against humanity. In either case, the priority is to end the bloodshed and ensure justice for the victims. The label of genocide is more than just a word -- but the world must not let arguments over that label delay the interventions needed to save lives. The ICJ noted that by late January *"many Palestinians in the Gaza Strip have no access to the most basic foodstuffs, potable water, electricity... or medicines"*[hrw.org](https://www.hrw.org/report/2024/12/19/extermination-and-acts-genocide/israel-deliberately-depriving-palestinians-gaza#:~:text=measures%20were%20adopted%20as%20part,14) -- essentially affirming the severity of conditions. Despite this world court order, **no significant improvement followed**. Observers on the ground (including two U.S. senators who visited the Rafah crossing in January) reported miles of aid trucks waiting for Israeli clearance while **Israelis imposed time-consuming checks and arbitrary rejections** of essential items like water filters and medical kits[congress.gov](https://www.congress.gov/118/crec/2024/03/20/170/49/modified/CREC-2024-03-20-pt1-PgS2479-2.htm#:~:text=Crossing%20from%20Egypt%20to%20learn,profound%20humanitarian%20crisis%20that%20was). These inspections often caused **weeks-long delays**, and if any single item in a truck was flagged, the entire cargo would be turned back[congress.gov](https://www.congress.gov/118/crec/2024/03/20/170/49/modified/CREC-2024-03-20-pt1-PgS2479-2.htm#:~:text=Crossing%20from%20Egypt%20to%20learn,and%20no%20Israeli%20interest%20in). Such obstructive practices meant that in January the flow of aid was nowhere close to meeting Gaza's needs. In Sanders' words, *"our [Senators'] colleagues saw a process that was completely broken and no Israeli interest in fixing it, despite the profound humanitarian crisis"*[congress.gov](https://www.congress.gov/118/crec/2024/03/20/170/49/modified/CREC-2024-03-20-pt1-PgS2479-2.htm#:~:text=Crossing%20from%20Egypt%20to%20learn,profound%20humanitarian%20crisis%20that%20was).

**February--March 2024: First Starvation Deaths and Famine Imminent.** As fighting shifted primarily to the north of Gaza, conditions there became virtually unsurvivable. By February 2024, **reports emerged of families eating leaves and animal feed** to stay alive[congress.gov](https://www.congress.gov/118/crec/2024/03/20/170/49/modified/CREC-2024-03-20-pt1-PgS2479-2.htm#:~:text=secure%20food%2C%20were%20shot%20by,or%20trampled%20in%20the%20chaos). With health systems shattered, many deaths likely went unrecorded, but aid groups started documenting **children dying from malnutrition and dehydration** by mid/late February[congress.gov](https://www.congress.gov/118/crec/2024/03/20/170/49/modified/CREC-2024-03-20-pt1-PgS2479-2.htm#:~:text=secure%20food%2C%20were%20shot%20by,or%20trampled%20in%20the%20chaos). On the ground, **hundreds of desperate civilians broke into aid warehouses** or distribution sites in search of food; tragically, in some instances **starving people were shot by Israeli troops or trampled in chaos** during such scrambles for food[congress.gov](https://www.congress.gov/118/crec/2024/03/20/170/49/modified/CREC-2024-03-20-pt1-PgS2479-2.htm#:~:text=The%20situation%2C%20as%20a%20result%2C,or%20trampled%20in%20the%20chaos)[congress.gov](https://www.congress.gov/118/crec/2024/03/20/170/49/modified/CREC-2024-03-20-pt1-PgS2479-2.htm#:~:text=U,to%20read%20the%20headline%20here). In early March, a comprehensive food security analysis (the **IPC -- Integrated Food Security Phase Classification** report) was released by UN agencies and partners. Its headline was dire: *"**Famine is imminent** as 1.1 million people -- half of Gaza's population -- experience catastrophic food insecurity"*[congress.gov](https://www.congress.gov/118/crec/2024/03/20/170/49/modified/CREC-2024-03-20-pt1-PgS2479-2.htm#:~:text=called%20the%20IPC,to%20read%20the%20headline%20here). The IPC warned that **over 200,000 people were already starving to death**, and if nothing changed, *"more than a million people could starve"* in the coming months[congress.gov](https://www.congress.gov/118/crec/2024/03/20/170/49/modified/CREC-2024-03-20-pt1-PgS2479-2.htm#:~:text=called%20the%20IPC,to%20read%20the%20headline%20here). By that time at least **31 people (including 27 children)** were confirmed to have **died of starvation or dehydration**, with the *real toll likely much higher* due to inaccessible areas[congress.gov](https://www.congress.gov/118/crec/2024/03/20/170/49/modified/CREC-2024-03-20-pt1-PgS2479-2.htm#:~:text=starve,2%20years%20of%20age%20in). UNICEF reported in March that *nearly 1 in 3 children under 2 years old in northern Gaza was suffering from acute malnutrition*[congress.gov](https://www.congress.gov/118/crec/2024/03/20/170/49/modified/CREC-2024-03-20-pt1-PgS2479-2.htm#:~:text=as%20aid%20organizations%20and%20medical,consumes%20itself%20from%20within%2C%20and) -- an extremely high rate that indicates many young children's lives were at imminent risk. In response to these developments, the ICJ in late March **reaffirmed and expanded its provisional orders**, instructing Israel not only to cease actions that risk genocide but also to **provide "unhindered" aid at scale -- food, water, fuel, and other essentials**[hrw.org](https://www.hrw.org/report/2024/12/19/extermination-and-acts-genocide/israel-deliberately-depriving-palestinians-gaza#:~:text=less%20than%20what%20is%20produced.). Again, these international legal directives were largely ignored on the ground[hrw.org](https://www.hrw.org/report/2024/12/19/extermination-and-acts-genocide/israel-deliberately-depriving-palestinians-gaza#:~:text=On%20March%2028%2C%20the%20ICJ,30). Israel's forces continued operations and siege tactics, and although some humanitarian convoys were allowed, they were **"nowhere near enough to reverse months of starvation"** or *"stave off a wave of deaths"*, as Senator Sanders noted in late March[congress.gov](https://www.congress.gov/118/crec/2024/03/20/170/49/modified/CREC-2024-03-20-pt1-PgS2479-2.htm#:~:text=coming%20in%20of%20children%20dying,assessment%20of%20the%20food%20crisis).

**April--July 2024: Famine Declared and Ongoing Blockade.** Despite warnings, the humanitarian situation continued to deteriorate into the spring of 2024. In **early May 2024**, the International Criminal Court (ICC) made a notable move: the ICC prosecutor obtained arrest warrants (under seal, later reported) or at least acknowledged investigations targeting top Israeli officials. By **May 2024**, Israeli Prime Minister **Netanyahu** and (former) Defense Minister **Gallant** became the **first-ever individuals formally accused by an international court of the war crime of deliberate starvation** of civilians[theguardian.com](https://www.theguardian.com/world/2025/aug/04/gaza-starvation-un-expert-michael-fakhri#:~:text=In%20May%2C%20the%20Israeli%20prime,which%20is%20a%20war%20crime). This underscored the gravity with which international jurists viewed the situation. In **July 2024**, a panel of UN experts (including Michael Fakhri) went a step further and **formally declared that a famine was occurring in Gaza**, after confirming the first documented **deaths from starvation**[theguardian.com](https://www.theguardian.com/world/2025/aug/04/gaza-starvation-un-expert-michael-fakhri#:~:text=In%20July%202024%2C%20a%20group,current%20siege%20in%20October%202023). This declaration aimed to galvanize global action, as famine is a technical classification that implies an extraordinary humanitarian catastrophe. The experts emphasized that **Israel's decades-long control over food and supplies** to Gaza -- which had left 80% of Gazans dependent on aid even before the war[theguardian.com](https://www.theguardian.com/world/2025/aug/04/gaza-starvation-un-expert-michael-fakhri#:~:text=In%20July%202024%2C%20a%20group,current%20siege%20in%20October%202023) -- set the stage for this disaster. They noted that Israel had *"systematically destroyed local food production (farms, orchards, fishing boats) and blocked aid, in violation of international law"*[theguardian.com](https://www.theguardian.com/world/2025/aug/04/gaza-starvation-un-expert-michael-fakhri#:~:text=Yet%20there%20has%20been%20little,in%20violation%20of%20international%20law), directly leading to famine conditions. By mid-2024, **famine was not an unintended side-effect** but rather the foreseeable result of a sustained siege. As Fakhri put it, *"Famine is always political, always predictable and always preventable... to frame the mass starvation as a consequence of just the most recent blockade is a misunderstanding. People don't all of a sudden starve... This is because they have been deliberately weakened for so long."*[theguardian.com](https://www.theguardian.com/world/2025/aug/04/gaza-starvation-un-expert-michael-fakhri#:~:text=inevitably%20leads%20to%20famine%20if,is%20taken%20to%20avoid%20it). In other words, Gaza's population had already been made highly vulnerable by years of blockade, and the 2023--24 "complete siege" was the lethal blow. Throughout this period, **Israel only "loosened or tightened its starvation machine" in response to external pressure**[theguardian.com](https://www.theguardian.com/world/2025/aug/04/gaza-starvation-un-expert-michael-fakhri#:~:text=%E2%80%9CBut%20to%20frame%20the%20mass,tuning%20this%20for%2025%20years.%E2%80%9D), without ever restoring adequate supplies. Indeed, data shows that after an initial 5-week total cutoff of fuel, from November 2023 through August 2024 Israel allowed only about **20% of the fuel needed** for essential humanitarian operations to enter Gaza[hrw.org](https://www.hrw.org/report/2024/12/19/extermination-and-acts-genocide/israel-deliberately-depriving-palestinians-gaza#:~:text=Israeli%20authorities%20also%20initially%20blocked,23). Water infrastructure remained crippled; even by September 2024, water flow from Israel was a fraction of pre-war levels and insufficient for basic needs[hrw.org](https://www.hrw.org/report/2024/12/19/extermination-and-acts-genocide/israel-deliberately-depriving-palestinians-gaza#:~:text=While%20Israeli%20authorities%20resumed%20piping,or%20destruction%20of%20water%20infrastructure)[hrw.org](https://www.hrw.org/report/2024/12/19/extermination-and-acts-genocide/israel-deliberately-depriving-palestinians-gaza#:~:text=As%20of%20September%202024%2C%20Israeli,the%20pipelines%2C%20desalination%20facilities%2C%20wells). The **ICJ issued further orders in May and again later in 2024**, each time pressing Israel to comply with preventing genocide and enabling aid[hrw.org](https://www.hrw.org/report/2024/12/19/extermination-and-acts-genocide/israel-deliberately-depriving-palestinians-gaza#:~:text=Israel%20was%20violating%20the%20Genocide,14)[hrw.org](https://www.hrw.org/report/2024/12/19/extermination-and-acts-genocide/israel-deliberately-depriving-palestinians-gaza#:~:text=less%20than%20what%20is%20produced.) -- to no avail. Israeli authorities openly flouted these legal injunctions[hrw.org](https://www.hrw.org/report/2024/12/19/extermination-and-acts-genocide/israel-deliberately-depriving-palestinians-gaza#:~:text=On%20March%2028%2C%20the%20ICJ,30)[hrw.org](https://www.hrw.org/report/2024/12/19/extermination-and-acts-genocide/israel-deliberately-depriving-palestinians-gaza#:~:text=Since%20that%20time%2C%20Israel%20has,15).

**Late 2024--2025: Escalation and Starvation Catastrophe.** By late 2024, Israel's military campaign had shifted but not ended. (Israeli ground forces withdrew from central/southern Gaza around May 2024, consolidating control in buffer zones, while continuing strikes elsewhere[hrw.org](https://www.hrw.org/report/2024/12/19/extermination-and-acts-genocide/israel-deliberately-depriving-palestinians-gaza#:~:text=Palestinians%20returning%20to%20Khan%20Younis,Ali%20Jadallah%2FAnadolu%20via%20Getty%20Images)[hrw.org](https://www.hrw.org/report/2024/12/19/extermination-and-acts-genocide/israel-deliberately-depriving-palestinians-gaza#:~:text=Because%20of%20the%20decimation%20of,7).) The **blockade, however, persisted** through 2024 and into 2025, with Israel using access to food and water as a lever of control. In early 2025, a change in U.S. leadership (President Donald Trump's return to office in January 2025) led to different dynamics: a ceasefire was briefly brokered, during which slightly more aid entered and **Israel established the Gaza Humanitarian Foundation (GHF)** to handle aid distribution from May 2025[theguardian.com](https://www.theguardian.com/world/2025/aug/04/gaza-starvation-un-expert-michael-fakhri#:~:text=The%20Gaza%20Humanitarian%20Foundation%20,was%20being%20diverted%20by%20Hamas). However, this move was widely condemned by the UN and NGOs as a cynical "weaponization of aid" -- the GHF replaced UN-run distribution centers with only a few tightly controlled hubs guarded by armed contractors[theguardian.com](https://www.theguardian.com/world/2025/aug/04/gaza-starvation-un-expert-michael-fakhri#:~:text=The%20Gaza%20Humanitarian%20Foundation%20,was%20being%20diverted%20by%20Hamas). On **March 2025**, Israel unilaterally broke a ceasefire and **reinstated the total blockade**, even harsher than before[theguardian.com](https://www.theguardian.com/world/2025/aug/04/gaza-starvation-un-expert-michael-fakhri#:~:text=Unicef%20is%20among%20multiple%20aid,starved%2C%20sick%20and%20weakened%20population). UNICEF and multiple aid agencies reported a **sharp escalation in malnutrition and starvation** from March 2025 onward[theguardian.com](https://www.theguardian.com/world/2025/aug/04/gaza-starvation-un-expert-michael-fakhri#:~:text=Unicef%20is%20among%20multiple%20aid,starved%2C%20sick%20and%20weakened%20population). By mid-2025, Gaza's health system documented more than **20,000 children hospitalized for acute malnutrition between April and mid-July 2025**[theguardian.com](https://www.theguardian.com/world/2025/aug/04/gaza-starvation-un-expert-michael-fakhri#:~:text=Now%2C%20widespread%20starvation%2C%20malnutrition%20and,the%20UN%20and%20aid%20groups) -- an astronomical number indicating the near-collapse of child health. The famine that experts warned about was now fully materializing: a July 2025 alert by the IPC stated the "**worst-case scenario of famine is currently playing out**" across Gaza[theguardian.com](https://www.theguardian.com/world/2025/aug/04/gaza-starvation-un-expert-michael-fakhri#:~:text=and%20aid%20groups). Widespread starvation and related disease had become leading causes of death. In fact, hunger-related deaths surged, with more than 20--30 people (especially children) reportedly dying each day from starvation or lack of medical care by summer 2025 (per NGO field reports). The **GHF aid distribution experiment turned deadly**: on June 1, 2025, Israeli soldiers opened fire on crowds at GHF aid sites, killing 32 people in one incident[theguardian.com](https://www.theguardian.com/world/2025/aug/04/gaza-starvation-un-expert-michael-fakhri#:~:text=The%20UN%20and%20hundreds%20of,territories%2C%20accusing%20them%20of%20antisemitism). In the following weeks, over **1,300 starving Palestinians were killed while trying to access food** at these militarized aid centers[theguardian.com](https://www.theguardian.com/world/2025/aug/04/gaza-starvation-un-expert-michael-fakhri#:~:text=The%20UN%20and%20hundreds%20of,territories%2C%20accusing%20them%20of%20antisemitism) -- essentially, people were *shot for being hungry*. The Israeli government dismissed these reports as "exaggerations" and blamed Hamas for any chaos[theguardian.com](https://www.theguardian.com/world/2025/aug/04/gaza-starvation-un-expert-michael-fakhri#:~:text=In%20a%20statement%2C%20GHF%20rejected,piloting%20now%2C%E2%80%9D%20a%20spokesperson%20said). Throughout 2025, Israel continued to bar independent investigators and journalists from entering Gaza, making it difficult to formally document the full scale of mortality[theguardian.com](https://www.theguardian.com/world/2025/aug/04/gaza-starvation-un-expert-michael-fakhri#:~:text=The%20Israeli%20government%20did%20not,respond%20to%20request%20for%20comment). Nonetheless, estimates by research groups put the **total Palestinian death toll** (from bombings, ground attacks, and now starvation) at **well over 60,000 by mid-2025**[theguardian.com](https://www.theguardian.com/world/2025/aug/04/gaza-starvation-un-expert-michael-fakhri#:~:text=The%20Israeli%20government%20did%20not,respond%20to%20request%20for%20comment), with some studies saying the real toll is significantly higher. By August 2025 -- nearly two years into the war -- UN officials like Michael Fakhri lamented that *"Israel has built the most efficient starvation machine you can imagine... Israel is starving Gaza. **It's genocide.** It's a crime against humanity. It's a war crime."*[theguardian.com](https://www.theguardian.com/world/2025/aug/04/gaza-starvation-un-expert-michael-fakhri#:~:text=The%20UN%20expert%20who%20first,at%20the%20horror%20now%20unfolding)[theguardian.com](https://www.theguardian.com/world/2025/aug/04/gaza-starvation-un-expert-michael-fakhri#:~:text=%E2%80%9CIsrael%20is%20starving%20Gaza,warnings%20and%20predictions%20were%20ignored).

**Summary of Humanitarian Findings:** Over the span from Oct 2023 to late 2025, the data shows a **consistent pattern of extreme deprivation in Gaza caused by Israeli policy**. Despite ample warning from international monitors at each stage (Nov 2023, Jan 2024, Mar 2024, etc.), Israel **did not substantially relent** in its siege tactics. Limited pauses or minor increases in aid were **insufficient and temporary**, and often tied to political conditions (e.g. hostage releases) rather than humanitarian necessity[congress.gov](https://www.congress.gov/118/crec/2024/03/20/170/49/modified/CREC-2024-03-20-pt1-PgS2479-2.htm#:~:text=secure%20food%2C%20were%20shot%20by,or%20trampled%20in%20the%20chaos)[hrw.org](https://www.hrw.org/report/2024/12/19/extermination-and-acts-genocide/israel-deliberately-depriving-palestinians-gaza#:~:text=,162). Crucially, whenever Israel was faced with calls to avert mass death, it either continued the blockade or even tightened it (as in March 2025). The result has been a progressive deterioration into what is now recognized as one of the worst humanitarian disasters of modern times[congress.gov](https://www.congress.gov/118/crec/2024/03/20/170/49/modified/CREC-2024-03-20-pt1-PgS2479-2.htm#:~:text=Mr,be%20complicit%20in). The *deliberate infliction of conditions of life calculated to bring about a population's destruction* -- one of the acts of genocide defined in Article II of the Genocide Convention -- appears to be exactly what the protracted siege represents[theguardian.com](https://www.theguardian.com/world/2025/aug/04/gaza-starvation-un-expert-michael-fakhri#:~:text=%E2%80%9CIsrael%20is%20starving%20Gaza,warnings%20and%20predictions%20were%20ignored)[theguardian.com](https://www.theguardian.com/world/2025/aug/04/gaza-starvation-un-expert-michael-fakhri#:~:text=The%20UN%20expert%20who%20first,at%20the%20horror%20now%20unfolding). Whether one labels it genocide or not, the factual record of an **"artificial famine"** imposed on an entire civilian population is starkly clear and unprecedented in its speed and scale (millions pushed to the brink of starvation in a matter of months[theguardian.com](https://www.theguardian.com/world/2025/aug/04/gaza-starvation-un-expert-michael-fakhri#:~:text=In%20an%20interview%20with%20the,%E2%80%9D)).

Next, we turn to the **statements and rhetoric of Israeli leaders** over this same period, to assess the question of **intent** -- a critical component in determining genocide equivalence.

Israeli Officials' Rhetoric and Policy Statements Indicative of Intent (Oct 2023 -- Present)
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

A thorough review of public statements by Israeli politicians and military leaders from October 2023 onward reveals a **widely documented pattern of dehumanizing language and explicit calls for collective punishment** of Gazans. Such statements are a key piece of evidence in determining whether the *intent* behind Israel's actions was to **destroy, in whole or in part, the Palestinian people in Gaza** -- which would meet the definition of genocidal intent[aljazeera.com](https://www.aljazeera.com/opinions/2024/1/14/intent-in-the-genocide-case-against-israel-is-not-hard-to-prove#:~:text=The%20crime%20of%20genocide%20has,is%20indeed%20carrying%20out%20genocide)[aljazeera.com](https://www.aljazeera.com/opinions/2024/1/14/intent-in-the-genocide-case-against-israel-is-not-hard-to-prove#:~:text=Intention%20is%20usually%20harder%20to,African%20legal%20team%20pointed%20out). Below, we compile and analyze a timeline of notable statements, showing how the rhetoric evolved (or persisted) over time:

-   **Oct 7, 2023 -- "City of Evil":** On the very day the war began, Prime Minister **Benjamin Netanyahu** addressed the nation and framed the conflict in absolutist, good-versus-evil terms. He referred to Gaza as *"the city of evil"*[aljazeera.com](https://www.aljazeera.com/opinions/2024/1/14/intent-in-the-genocide-case-against-israel-is-not-hard-to-prove#:~:text=Israeli%20Defence%20Minister%20Yoav%20Gallant,civilisation%20against%20barbarism%2C%E2%80%9D%20he%20said) and vowed to retaliate with unprecedented force. This set the tone by portraying the entire area (and by extension its population) as a malevolent entity. Such wording laid groundwork for treating all Gazans as part of the enemy.

-   **Oct 9, 2023 -- "Human Animals" and Total Siege:** As noted earlier, Defense Minister **Yoav Gallant** announced the complete siege on this date. In doing so, he used explicitly dehumanizing language: *"We are fighting **human animals** and we are acting accordingly."*[congress.gov](https://www.congress.gov/118/crec/2024/03/20/170/49/modified/CREC-2024-03-20-pt1-PgS2479-2.htm#:~:text=U,He%20said) Gallant's choice of words -- labeling Palestinians as sub-human -- is a textbook example of **incitement language** that often precedes or accompanies genocidal campaigns (historically, persecuted groups have been called "vermin," "cockroaches," etc., to justify their elimination). Gallant's statement was broadcast widely and signaled to Israeli forces and the public that no distinction would be made between militants and civilians ("acting accordingly" toward *"human animals"* implies brutal treatment of all). Along with this, his declaration "no food, no water, no fuel...everything is closed" was not just a policy but effectively a *threat to starve* the entire population[congress.gov](https://www.congress.gov/118/crec/2024/03/20/170/49/modified/CREC-2024-03-20-pt1-PgS2479-2.htm#:~:text=U,He%20said).

-   **Oct 10--12, 2023 -- Calls to Cut Off All Aid:** Other officials quickly amplified the siege policy. Then-Energy Minister **Israel Katz** bragged on social media about severing utilities, saying *"It's over. Without fuel, even the local [facilities won't run]..."* (post on Oct 10) and on **Oct 12** asserted that *no humanitarian assistance should be allowed into Gaza*[hrw.org](https://www.hrw.org/report/2024/12/19/extermination-and-acts-genocide/israel-deliberately-depriving-palestinians-gaza#:~:text=On%20October%2012%2C%20Katz%20asserted,Israel%20was%20blocking%20humanitarian%20assistance)[hrw.org](https://www.hrw.org/report/2024/12/19/extermination-and-acts-genocide/israel-deliberately-depriving-palestinians-gaza#:~:text=match%20at%20L5518%20,accessed%20August%2028%2C%202024). He later stated unequivocally, *"I am vehemently opposed to lifting the blockade and letting goods into Gaza for humanitarian reasons"*, prioritizing Israeli hostage families' feelings over the survival of Gaza's civilians[hrw.org](https://www.hrw.org/report/2024/12/19/extermination-and-acts-genocide/israel-deliberately-depriving-palestinians-gaza#:~:text=match%20at%20L1144%20,161). Similarly, **Itamar Ben-Gvir** (National Security Minister) was openly celebrating the blockade; on **Oct 17, 2023**, as mentioned, he wrote *"not an ounce of humanitarian aid"* should enter, only bombs[hrw.org](https://www.hrw.org/report/2024/12/19/extermination-and-acts-genocide/israel-deliberately-depriving-palestinians-gaza#:~:text=,162). These statements show **early, high-level intent to inflict collective punishment** by starving Gaza's people unless political demands were met (an approach fundamentally at odds with international law). Far from hiding this intent, these ministers broadcast it proudly. This abundance of *explicit* statements so soon after Oct 7 is notable -- as genocide scholars have pointed out, *"in the case of Israel's assault on Gaza...the intent has been laid bare by an ample amount of evidence"*, with hundreds of statements compiled in databases showing incitement[aljazeera.com](https://www.aljazeera.com/opinions/2024/1/14/intent-in-the-genocide-case-against-israel-is-not-hard-to-prove#:~:text=Intent%20in%20the%20genocide%20case,is%20not%20hard%20to%20prove)[aljazeera.com](https://www.aljazeera.com/opinions/2024/1/14/intent-in-the-genocide-case-against-israel-is-not-hard-to-prove#:~:text=In%20arguing%20the%20case%2C%20they,the%20genocidal%20destruction%20of%20Gaza).

-   **Mid/Late Oct 2023 -- Extremist Incitement from Public Figures:** In addition to official cabinet members, other Israeli public figures made even more extreme calls. For example, a senior Israeli military reservist and former Knesset member, **Moshe Feiglin**, reportedly suggested using a nuclear bomb on Gaza City if Hamas did not surrender, likening it to the U.S. nuking Hiroshima (this was widely reported in mid-October 2023). Another lawmaker, **Ariel Kallner (Likud)**, posted that *"this is a time for a Nakba"* -- calling for a repeat of the 1948 mass expulsion of Palestinians, but *"a Nakba that will dwarf the original"* (implying even greater scale)[mecouncil.org](https://mecouncil.org/blog_posts/israels-admission-of-genocide/#:~:text=Israel%27s%20Admission%20of%20Genocide%20,genocide%20to%20be%20guilty). Such rhetoric clearly urges the **mass displacement or destruction** of the Palestinian population as a whole. While these particular statements came from individuals outside the core war cabinet, they contributed to an atmosphere in which genocidal language was normalized in Israeli discourse after Oct 7. **No significant Israeli government voices repudiated these statements at the time**. On the contrary, the prevailing sentiment in Israeli media and politics was one of **vengeance and unchecked warfare** -- for instance, Defense Ministry advisor **Gal Luft** wrote that Gaza should be "returned to the stone age," and President **Isaac Herzog** argued that *"it's an entire nation out there [in Gaza] that is responsible"*, rejecting the idea of innocent civilians[aljazeera.com](https://www.aljazeera.com/opinions/2024/1/14/intent-in-the-genocide-case-against-israel-is-not-hard-to-prove#:~:text=genocide)[aljazeera.com](https://www.aljazeera.com/opinions/2024/1/14/intent-in-the-genocide-case-against-israel-is-not-hard-to-prove#:~:text=Israeli%20Defence%20Minister%20Yoav%20Gallant,civilisation%20against%20barbarism%2C%E2%80%9D%20he%20said). Herzog's comment on **Oct 12, 2023** (during a press briefing) effectively **blamed all Gazans collectively for Hamas's actions**, undermining the principle of civilian immunity. This collective attribution of guilt laid a justificatory premise for the large-scale destruction to come.

-   **November 2023 -- Framing Gaza's People as "The Enemy".** As Israeli strikes caused mounting civilian casualties, Israeli leaders doubled down on rhetoric that **erased the civilian/combatant distinction**. A pivotal concept invoked was the Biblical story of **Amalek** -- an ancient enemy that, in scripture, God commands to annihilate utterly. Israeli Prime Minister Netanyahu and others began privately and publicly comparing Hamas (and by extension Gaza) to *"Amalek"*. For example, on **October 28, 2023**, Netanyahu delivered a speech where he explicitly likened the Palestinians to Amalek[aljazeera.com](https://www.aljazeera.com/opinions/2024/1/14/intent-in-the-genocide-case-against-israel-is-not-hard-to-prove#:~:text=Image%3A%20Israeli%20Prime%20Minister%20Benjamin,Netanyahu). By invoking this, they cast the war in existential and even religious terms: *"This is a battle of civilization against barbarism,"* Netanyahu said on another occasion[aljazeera.com](https://www.aljazeera.com/opinions/2024/1/14/intent-in-the-genocide-case-against-israel-is-not-hard-to-prove#:~:text=Israeli%20Defence%20Minister%20Yoav%20Gallant,civilisation%20against%20barbarism%2C%E2%80%9D%20he%20said), painting Gaza's population as "barbarians" and "monsters" in a fight to save "Western civilisation"[aljazeera.com](https://www.aljazeera.com/opinions/2024/1/14/intent-in-the-genocide-case-against-israel-is-not-hard-to-prove#:~:text=October%209,civilisation%20against%20barbarism%2C%E2%80%9D%20he%20said)[aljazeera.com](https://www.aljazeera.com/opinions/2024/1/14/intent-in-the-genocide-case-against-israel-is-not-hard-to-prove#:~:text=Israeli%20President%20Isaac%20Herzog%20said,an%20empire%20of%20evil%E2%80%9D). President Herzog on **Dec 5, 2023** echoed that line: *"This is a war to really, truly save Western civilisation... from an empire of evil"*[aljazeera.com](https://www.aljazeera.com/opinions/2024/1/14/intent-in-the-genocide-case-against-israel-is-not-hard-to-prove#:~:text=Israeli%20President%20Isaac%20Herzog%20said,an%20empire%20of%20evil%E2%80%9D). These statements go far beyond targeting a militant group; they cast the **entire Palestinian population in Gaza as a malevolent monolith** ("empire of evil," "barbarians") that must be vanquished for the sake of civilization. Such language is strongly **indicative of genocidal intent**, as it posits that **the elimination of the group is a righteous, necessary act** for self-preservation of the perpetrator's society[aljazeera.com](https://www.aljazeera.com/opinions/2024/1/14/intent-in-the-genocide-case-against-israel-is-not-hard-to-prove#:~:text=This%20crude%20and%20dangerous%20weaponisation,genocide%20on%20Palestinians%20in%20Gaza). Genocide scholars note that perpetrators often perceive the victimized group as an existential threat that must be destroyed[aljazeera.com](https://www.aljazeera.com/opinions/2024/1/14/intent-in-the-genocide-case-against-israel-is-not-hard-to-prove#:~:text=This%20crude%20and%20dangerous%20weaponisation,genocide%20on%20Palestinians%20in%20Gaza) -- exactly how these Israeli leaders were describing Gaza's people in late 2023.

-   **December 2023 -- "Total Destruction" as a Goal:** By late December, Israeli officials were openly discussing Gaza's fate in terms of **absolute destruction**. In a December 24 press conference, Netanyahu spoke of the war as *"not only Israel against these barbarians, but civilisation against barbarism,"* pointedly using the word "monsters" for Gazans and leaving *"no doubt that saving 'Western civilisation' requires the **total destruction** of the Palestinians in Gaza,"* as analysts observed[aljazeera.com](https://www.aljazeera.com/opinions/2024/1/14/intent-in-the-genocide-case-against-israel-is-not-hard-to-prove#:~:text=colonised%20%E2%80%9Csavages%E2%80%9D)[aljazeera.com](https://www.aljazeera.com/opinions/2024/1/14/intent-in-the-genocide-case-against-israel-is-not-hard-to-prove#:~:text=Netanyahu%20and%20other%20senior%20Israeli,destroyed%20%E2%80%93%20and%20as%20Nazis). Around the same time, Israel's Foreign Minister **Eli Cohen** and Defense Minister Gallant both used the phrase *"Gaza will be **a city of tents**"* (meaning all permanent buildings would be flattened). Indeed, an IDF spokesperson stated that the **military's aim was "damage, not accuracy"**, implying that widescale obliteration of neighborhoods was intentional, regardless of civilian presence. All of this rhetoric and action (the leveling of large parts of Gaza City and northern Gaza by year's end) indicate an **intent to destroy Gaza as a viable community**. Importantly, this was accompanied by **forced displacement**: 90% of Gaza's entire population was pushed into the southernmost zone by January[aljazeera.com](https://www.aljazeera.com/opinions/2024/1/14/intent-in-the-genocide-case-against-israel-is-not-hard-to-prove#:~:text=The%20mass%20killing%20of%20more,the%20damage%20and%20destruction%20of), an exodus driven by bombing and leaflets telling people to evacuate. The *genocidal pattern* of "**forcibly transferring** children or other members of the group" (another act under the Genocide Convention) can be seen in the mass displacement, especially since many did not survive the journey or found no safety even after moving. Israeli officials like Gallant described these population movements as permanent, suggesting Gaza's people might never be allowed to return to their homes (indeed many neighborhoods were systematically razed).

-   **Jan--Feb 2024 -- Boasts of Starvation and Continued Incitement:** Into 2024, even as the humanitarian toll became clearer to the world, some Israeli leaders showed *no compunction about the suffering*. **Itamar Ben-Gvir**, for example, proudly posted on **Feb 2, 2024** (123 days into the war) that he *"opposes the introduction of any humanitarian aid to the killers from Gaza"*[hrw.org](https://www.hrw.org/report/2024/12/19/extermination-and-acts-genocide/israel-deliberately-depriving-palestinians-gaza#:~:text=match%20at%20L5600%20,%E2%80%9D) -- essentially celebrating the starvation strategy as a success. This was nearly four months into the siege, at which point many children were already malnourished, yet a senior minister's policy was still **zero aid**. Also in early 2024, Finance Minister **Bezalel Smotrich** (a far-right member of the war cabinet) continued to make genocidal statements. On **August 5, 2024** -- nearly a year into the war -- Smotrich reiterated that *Israel would be justified in depriving the civilian population of water*[hrw.org](https://www.hrw.org/report/2024/12/19/extermination-and-acts-genocide/israel-deliberately-depriving-palestinians-gaza#:~:text=population%20of%20Gaza,17). He argued that as long as Hamas remained, any measure, including cutting water to 2 million people, was legitimate. This persistence of rhetoric, *even after* global accusations of genocide had surfaced, indicates an **entrenchment of the genocidal mindset** rather than an aberration. In the words of the genocide scholar Raz Segal, Israeli officials have been *"explicit and unashamed about their genocidal intent"* in Gaza[aljazeera.com](https://www.aljazeera.com/opinions/2024/1/14/intent-in-the-genocide-case-against-israel-is-not-hard-to-prove#:~:text=In%20arguing%20the%20case%2C%20they,the%20genocidal%20destruction%20of%20Gaza)[aljazeera.com](https://www.aljazeera.com/opinions/2024/1/14/intent-in-the-genocide-case-against-israel-is-not-hard-to-prove#:~:text=The%20Law%20for%20Palestine%20database,%E2%80%9D), emboldened by what they perceive as impunity and moral justification.

-   **Admissions of Strategy vs. Denials of Genocide:** There is a telling duality in Israeli officials' communications. Internally or in Hebrew, some have been candid about harsh measures, while externally there have been denials. For instance, as noted, **Netanyahu admitted in January 2024** that aid was being kept to the "minimum" necessary[congress.gov](https://www.congress.gov/118/crec/2024/03/20/170/49/modified/CREC-2024-03-20-pt1-PgS2479-2.htm#:~:text=Palestinian%20lives,N). Yet, when confronted with genocide accusations later, Israeli leaders denied intending to starve or exterminate civilians. In **mid-2025** as images of emaciated children were circulating globally, **Netanyahu stated**: *"There is **no policy of starvation in Gaza. There is no starvation in Gaza."*[theguardian.com](https://www.theguardian.com/world/2025/aug/04/gaza-starvation-un-expert-michael-fakhri#:~:text=Despite%20stark%20images%20of%20skeletal,%E2%80%9D). This statement is outright false given extensive UN documentation to the contrary, but it shows Israel's top leader trying to publicly reject the genocide label. Similarly, Israeli officials have insisted that any civilian deaths are "unfortunate collateral" or blamed Hamas for using people as shields. However, these **post-facto denials** ring hollow against the backdrop of their documented actions and earlier words. The Israeli government's *own data and actions* undermine the claim -- for example, Israel's ambassador to the UN acknowledged that they allowed "some" aid but not enough to meet needs (implicitly confirming use of deprivation). Moreover, Israeli spokespeople repeatedly said things like *"Gazans have only themselves to blame"* or that *"the responsibility is on Hamas if people starve"*, which again suggests **knowledge that people were indeed starving** and a decision to let it continue as a pressure tactic. The international community has largely rejected these excuses. By late 2024, even **Amnesty International** concluded that *"Israel has carried out acts prohibited under the Genocide Convention, with the specific intent to destroy part of the Palestinian population"*[amnesty.org](https://www.amnesty.org/en/latest/news/2024/12/amnesty-international-concludes-israel-is-committing-genocide-against-palestinians-in-gaza/#:~:text=%E2%80%9CAmnesty%20International%27s%20report%20demonstrates%20that,with%20the%20specific%20intent). A resolution by the **International Association of Genocide Scholars (IAGS)** in mid-2024 affirmed that Israel's actions in Gaza meet the legal definition of genocide[theguardian.com](https://www.theguardian.com/world/2025/sep/01/israel-committing-genocide-in-gaza-worlds-top-scholars-on-the-say#:~:text=,of%20members%20who%20voted). Such conclusions are drawn from the very statements and policies we have detailed -- in particular, the **systematic calls by Israeli leaders to treat all Gazans as the enemy and to withhold basics of life** are seen as clear evidence of *genocidal intent*[aljazeera.com](https://www.aljazeera.com/opinions/2024/1/14/intent-in-the-genocide-case-against-israel-is-not-hard-to-prove#:~:text=UN%20Convention%20on%20the%20Prevention,African%20legal%20team%20pointed%20out)[aljazeera.com](https://www.aljazeera.com/opinions/2024/1/14/intent-in-the-genocide-case-against-israel-is-not-hard-to-prove#:~:text=2023,the%20genocidal%20destruction%20of%20Gaza).

-   **Incitement Themes -- Summary:** The Israeli rhetoric since October 7, 2023 has featured classic hallmarks of incitement to genocide: **dehumanization** (e.g. "human animals"[congress.gov](https://www.congress.gov/118/crec/2024/03/20/170/49/modified/CREC-2024-03-20-pt1-PgS2479-2.htm#:~:text=U,He%20said)), **demonization** (branding the entire group as "evil" or "Nazis" in some comparisons[aljazeera.com](https://www.aljazeera.com/opinions/2024/1/14/intent-in-the-genocide-case-against-israel-is-not-hard-to-prove#:~:text=Netanyahu%20and%20other%20senior%20Israeli,destroyed%20%E2%80%93%20and%20as%20Nazis)), **accusation in a mirror** (portraying the victim group as an existential threat -- "they'll destroy us if we don't destroy them"), **calls for destruction or expulsion** ("Nakba 2.0", "wipe out Gaza", "no mercy"), and **justification through self-defense or divine mandate** (invoking Amalek, righteous vengeance). These elements have been uttered by individuals at the highest levels of Israel's government and military. The consistency and abundance of such statements over the span of the conflict greatly strengthen the case that the atrocities in Gaza are not just *excesses of war* but the result of a *conscious policy to destroy a substantial part of the Palestinian population*. Indeed, a **comprehensive database by the group Law for Palestine has documented over 500 examples** of Israeli political or military figures using genocidal language since the war began[aljazeera.com](https://www.aljazeera.com/opinions/2024/1/14/intent-in-the-genocide-case-against-israel-is-not-hard-to-prove#:~:text=In%20arguing%20the%20case%2C%20they,the%20genocidal%20destruction%20of%20Gaza). In court, direct evidence of intent is rare in genocide cases; often intent is inferred from patterns of action. However, here we have both patterns of action *and* explicit words aligning with those actions. This convergence is what led experts like Professor Penny Green to state: *"Intent [in this case] is not hard to prove"*[aljazeera.com](https://www.aljazeera.com/opinions/2024/1/14/intent-in-the-genocide-case-against-israel-is-not-hard-to-prove#:~:text=Intent%20in%20the%20genocide%20case,is%20not%20hard%20to%20prove)[aljazeera.com](https://www.aljazeera.com/opinions/2024/1/14/intent-in-the-genocide-case-against-israel-is-not-hard-to-prove#:~:text=Intention%20is%20usually%20harder%20to,African%20legal%20team%20pointed%20out).

Conclusion: Equivalence vs Non-Equivalence -- Where Does the Evidence Point?
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

Bringing the data together, we assess whether Israel's conduct in Gaza from Oct 2023 to the present constitutes **genocide-equivalence** (i.e. meets the criteria for genocide) or not.

**On the side of "genocide-equivalence", the evidence is substantial:** Israel's actions have included **killing tens of thousands of Palestinians, causing serious bodily and mental harm to hundreds of thousands more, and deliberately inflicting conditions of life (through blockade and destruction of infrastructure) calculated to bring about the physical destruction of the group in part**. These correspond to several acts defined as genocidal in Article II of the Genocide Convention[aljazeera.com](https://www.aljazeera.com/opinions/2024/1/14/intent-in-the-genocide-case-against-israel-is-not-hard-to-prove#:~:text=The%20crime%20of%20genocide%20has,is%20indeed%20carrying%20out%20genocide)[aljazeera.com](https://www.aljazeera.com/opinions/2024/1/14/intent-in-the-genocide-case-against-israel-is-not-hard-to-prove#:~:text=The%20mass%20killing%20of%20more,the%20damage%20and%20destruction%20of). Crucially, the **intent** to achieve the destruction of a significant part of the Palestinian population can be inferred from:

-   The *consistent policy choices* (e.g. maintaining a siege even as famine unfolded, targeting civilian infrastructure, mass displacement), and

-   The *candid statements* of leaders advocating for extreme collective punishment and even annihilation.

Far from being hidden, the intention was often openly declared: *"Israel is starving Gaza... It's genocide,"* said the UN food rapporteur in early 2024, frustrated that his warnings were ignored[theguardian.com](https://www.theguardian.com/world/2025/aug/04/gaza-starvation-un-expert-michael-fakhri#:~:text=%E2%80%9CIsrael%20is%20starving%20Gaza,warnings%20and%20predictions%20were%20ignored). By early 2024 the **ICJ** had already noted the plausibility of genocide happening[alhaq.org](https://www.alhaq.org/FAI-Unit/26257.html#:~:text=On%2026%20January%202024%2C%20the,Palestinians%20in%20the%20West%20Bank), and by late 2024 the **UN experts and major human rights organizations (HRW, Amnesty)** all pointed to the same conclusion. Indeed, **Human Rights Watch's December 2024 report** explicitly labeled Israel's deprivation of water as **"acts of genocide"**, using the term in its title[hrw.org](https://www.hrw.org/report/2024/12/19/extermination-and-acts-genocide/israel-deliberately-depriving-palestinians-gaza#:~:text=December%2019%2C%202024)[hrw.org](https://www.hrw.org/report/2024/12/19/extermination-and-acts-genocide/israel-deliberately-depriving-palestinians-gaza#:~:text=Since%20October%202023%2C%20Israeli%20authorities,survival%20in%20the%20Gaza%20Strip). The report documented how Israeli forces not only cut off water but even systematically **destroyed water infrastructure** (e.g. demolishing solar panels of desalination plants, shooting water technicians) to ensure the population's suffering[hrw.org](https://www.hrw.org/report/2024/12/19/extermination-and-acts-genocide/israel-deliberately-depriving-palestinians-gaza#:~:text=deprived%20Palestinians%20in%20Gaza%20of,8)[hrw.org](https://www.hrw.org/report/2024/12/19/extermination-and-acts-genocide/israel-deliberately-depriving-palestinians-gaza#:~:text=Israeli%20authorities%20made%20clear%20their,10). This goes beyond military necessity and squarely into intent to harm the population's survival.

Furthermore, the **scale and systematic nature** of the atrocities in Gaza reinforce the genocide-equivalence argument. When 1 million children are malnourished and hundreds of thousands are at risk of starvation as a direct result of intentional blockade[congress.gov](https://www.congress.gov/118/crec/2024/03/20/170/49/modified/CREC-2024-03-20-pt1-PgS2479-2.htm#:~:text=called%20the%20IPC,to%20read%20the%20headline%20here)[congress.gov](https://www.congress.gov/118/crec/2024/03/20/170/49/modified/CREC-2024-03-20-pt1-PgS2479-2.htm#:~:text=as%20aid%20organizations%20and%20medical,consumes%20itself%20from%20within%2C%20and), it is hard to argue this is anything but an effort to destroy a substantial part of a national group (the Palestinians of Gaza). The fact that Israeli officials themselves have described their campaign in existential terms -- even as a *"holy war"* of civilization -- shows that they view the **elimination or permanent incapacitation of Gaza's population as the end goal**[aljazeera.com](https://www.aljazeera.com/opinions/2024/1/14/intent-in-the-genocide-case-against-israel-is-not-hard-to-prove#:~:text=Netanyahu%20and%20other%20senior%20Israeli,destroyed%20%E2%80%93%20and%20as%20Nazis)[aljazeera.com](https://www.aljazeera.com/opinions/2024/1/14/intent-in-the-genocide-case-against-israel-is-not-hard-to-prove#:~:text=This%20crude%20and%20dangerous%20weaponisation,genocide%20on%20Palestinians%20in%20Gaza). Genocide scholars often talk about the **"intent to destroy" manifesting as an intent to eliminate the group's societal existence**; Israel's stated aim to reduce Gaza to "tent cities" and refusal to allow basic life-sustaining supplies fits this pattern[aljazeera.com](https://www.aljazeera.com/opinions/2024/1/14/intent-in-the-genocide-case-against-israel-is-not-hard-to-prove#:~:text=Israeli%20Defence%20Minister%20Yoav%20Gallant,civilisation%20against%20barbarism%2C%E2%80%9D%20he%20said)[hrw.org](https://www.hrw.org/report/2024/12/19/extermination-and-acts-genocide/israel-deliberately-depriving-palestinians-gaza#:~:text=Since%20that%20time%2C%20Israeli%20authorities,August%205%2C%20for%20example%2C%20Israeli).

**On the side of "non-equivalence" (not genocide), what evidence exists?** Israel officially denies that it is committing genocide. The arguments used include: **(1)** Israel's stated *military objective* is the destruction of Hamas, not the Palestinian people per se, and any civilian deaths or suffering are, in their view, unintentional side-effects of lawful warfare against a terrorist organization. **(2)** Israeli spokespeople have pointed out that they have allowed some humanitarian aid into Gaza (for example, as of mid-2025 they highlight that hundreds of trucks have entered, though far short of needs) and that they even set up the GHF aid distribution (albeit with controversy). **(3)** Israel and its allies claim that *"starvation is not policy"* and blame Hamas for hoarding supplies or using civilians as shields[theguardian.com](https://www.theguardian.com/world/2025/aug/04/gaza-starvation-un-expert-michael-fakhri#:~:text=Despite%20stark%20images%20of%20skeletal,%E2%80%9D). They also emphasize that Israel has urged civilians to evacuate combat zones (implying a desire to avoid killing them, although in practice nowhere in Gaza was truly safe).

However, when weighed against the collected evidence, these defenses appear weak. The allowance of *minimal aid* was explicitly tied to achieving war goals (Netanyahu's own words: only the "minimal aid" necessary would be given[congress.gov](https://www.congress.gov/118/crec/2024/03/20/170/49/modified/CREC-2024-03-20-pt1-PgS2479-2.htm#:~:text=Palestinian%20lives,N)). Israeli officials from the war cabinet (like Katz and Ben-Gvir) have repeatedly and very publicly opposed **any real relief** to the population[hrw.org](https://www.hrw.org/report/2024/12/19/extermination-and-acts-genocide/israel-deliberately-depriving-palestinians-gaza#:~:text=,162)[hrw.org](https://www.hrw.org/report/2024/12/19/extermination-and-acts-genocide/israel-deliberately-depriving-palestinians-gaza#:~:text=match%20at%20L5600%20,%E2%80%9D). The claim that Hamas's presence excuses cutting off babies and hospital patients from water and electricity does not hold under international law -- and Israel's actions (e.g. bombing evacuation convoys, continuing siege even in areas with no active combat) suggest the *intent goes beyond neutralizing Hamas*. In fact, **independent military experts note** that starving civilians has negligible direct impact on Hamas fighters who likely have stockpiles -- meaning the tactic's primary impact is indeed on the civilian population, which reinforces that **civilians were the target** of this pressure campaign[congress.gov](https://www.congress.gov/118/crec/2024/03/20/170/49/modified/CREC-2024-03-20-pt1-PgS2479-2.htm#:~:text=secure%20food%2C%20were%20shot%20by,or%20trampled%20in%20the%20chaos)[congress.gov](https://www.congress.gov/118/crec/2024/03/20/170/49/modified/CREC-2024-03-20-pt1-PgS2479-2.htm#:~:text=U,to%20read%20the%20headline%20here).

Even accepting Israel's goal was to destroy Hamas, the means chosen were so disproportionate and indiscriminate that they align with an intent to destroy **the broader Palestinian presence in Gaza**. As the Al Jazeera analysis by Segal and Green put it, *"the visible execution of genocide"* is there in the mass killing of over 23,000 Palestinians (by Jan 2024) and the laying waste to Gaza's cities[aljazeera.com](https://www.aljazeera.com/opinions/2024/1/14/intent-in-the-genocide-case-against-israel-is-not-hard-to-prove#:~:text=genocide). They argue that even if direct admissions of "we will kill them all" are rare, the **"ample amount of evidence"** of incitement and the extreme outcomes allow little ambiguity[aljazeera.com](https://www.aljazeera.com/opinions/2024/1/14/intent-in-the-genocide-case-against-israel-is-not-hard-to-prove#:~:text=UN%20Convention%20on%20the%20Prevention,African%20legal%20team%20pointed%20out)[aljazeera.com](https://www.aljazeera.com/opinions/2024/1/14/intent-in-the-genocide-case-against-israel-is-not-hard-to-prove#:~:text=2023,the%20genocidal%20destruction%20of%20Gaza).
